## 2. Latent best story (my ingredient-derived read, formed before reading the article)

**This is a "who steered the verdict" session, not an "inheriting-widow" one — and the ingredients point hard at Vic.**

The room convicted **Morgan** — and the evidence against Morgan is genuinely real (mor003 hammock blackmail-then-bribe; mor004 selling Marcus out to Taylor; ril002 the shell-company/insider-trading scheme he ran with Marcus; the flight from the country). Unlike the absent-scapegoat sessions, Morgan was *present* and *implicated*. So the gap here is not "the room blamed an innocent" — it is **WHO produced the verdict and what it bought them.**

Every ingredient converges on **Vic Kingsley as the room's hidden operator**:
- Vic is the **common thread in BOTH** opening accusation theories (Morgan/Vic, and Vic/Sarah) — the one name everyone's suspicion circled.
- Vic **cast the deciding vote against Morgan in the final minute**, breaking an alliance Morgan thought they had ("they had an agreement").
- The director's behavioral log is saturated with Vic *managing* people: shadowy-corner huddles with Morgan, "heavy negotiations" with Sam, repeated quiet conversations with Sam + Mel, the "I need BOTH of your companies" line to Remi (a man positioning his portfolio, not mourning), and the explosive "I can't believe you would take such actions!" at Morgan right before the vote — which reads as Vic *severing* a co-conspirator he was about to throw under the bus.
- **Vic's motive is the clearest and least examined.** He put $20M into NeurAI — a company the night's evidence proves is built on stolen code (zia004/rem004) with a founder who was self-destructing (blackmail, self-dosing, draining his wife). A dead Marcus with a controllable successor protects the investment; a live, spiraling Marcus is a liability. Vic profits from the death and from steering blame onto the fixer who knew where the financial bodies were buried.
- The **fake-account tells** (the director's strongest gift): the 'Remi' account has multiple fingerprints — "designed to frame Remi for murder"; the 'Marcus friend' account also has mixed fingerprints, possibly built to harvest contributions from Marcus's real allies. Someone with the *relationships to coordinate* manufactured a paper trail to muddy a competitor (Remi) and shape what the room saw. Vic — who "worked the room" and "controlled the money" — is the obvious coordinator.

So the latent thesis: **the room ran a murder trial; Vic ran a board maneuver inside it.** The convicted man (Morgan) is real but convenient — he's the one person whose testimony could implicate Vic's own financial-crime exposure, and Vic personally delivered the finishing vote. The throughline must read the verdict *against the man who produced it*: Morgan didn't necessarily run from a crime — he may have run from a **frame built by his own partner**, while the investor who profited most stays, smiling, and "does not need to run." A secondary true thread: the **manufactured evidence accounts** show the truth-finding infrastructure itself was weaponized to steer the conclusion.

## 5. Interaction-model contribution

**Failure-signals it adds.** Two of note:

Plus the **Ashe = she** invention is notable because Ashe is canonically they/them AND carries the byline. The fix is the standing one: harvest pronoun cues from Accusation/Notes prose ("his work being stolen") and treat them as authoritative over any default.

Secondary soft edges: the Feb 21 vs Feb 22 dateline slip; the source-inherited Nat roster inconsistency; the occasional un-re-attributed room-interior narration in a remote piece; era em-dashes.
