## 2. Latent best story (my ingredient-derived read, formed before reading the article)

**The gap:** the room voted **suicide** — the one verdict that makes every motive in the room irrelevant and requires no one to be guilty of anything — while **paying $1.77M to bury the evidence of how Marcus (and they) operated.** A suicide finding is the perfect product: no murder ⇒ no investigation into motive ⇒ no scrutiny of who profited. The money is the counter-narrative to the verdict.

The decisive ingredient structure:
- **Burial was a choice, not universal panic.** Morgan — who had plenty to hide — **refused to bury** (never seen with Blake; his damning tokens were exposed). So the players who *did* bury (Victoria, Derek, Sarah, "Princess D") self-selected as the people who most needed the books closed. Morgan is the control that proves the point.
- **Institutional power buried the deepest, and most anonymously.** The most expensive memories belong to **Victoria the VC** (vik001/vik002, $450K each) — the person who offered Alex Marcus's job ("He's out. You're in"), seen repeatedly at Blake's station — and they vanished into the two biggest **anonymous** accounts (Lily $685K, Billy $585K). The person with the most leverage over Marcus's succession spent the morning making her own tracks disappear behind sock-puppet names.
- **Sarah is the brazen counterpoint.** She buried under her **own name** and is the named target of a **$450K offer to betray Marcus (sab001)** she never cashed — while her *sympathetic* origin memory (sab002, resigned from Facebook to back Marcus's launch) sat exposed. The widow neither hid nor fully cashed in: a different, quieter signature than the anonymous aggregators.

**Thesis:** *A suicide verdict is what a room buys when everyone in it had a reason to want the investigation closed.* Run the throughline: the vote said "no one is responsible," but $1.77M changed hands to keep specific memories buried — and the biggest, most anonymous piles point at the people with the most institutional power (Victoria), while the lone holdout (Morgan) proves burial was a deliberate choice. The suicide isn't the story; the **price of the suicide** is.

## 5. Interaction-model contribution

**Success signal (qualified positive).** This is an encouraging data point that the pipeline could find a **read-against-the-verdict** thesis very early in the corpus: it correctly identified that a **suicide verdict + $1.7M of burial = a bought conclusion**, foregrounded the two anonymous piles, and — its best move — used **Morgan-the-holdout as the control case** that proves burial was a choice. When the director hands you the control case (Morgan), the model uses it well.

**Failure signals it adds to the taxonomy:**
1. **Pronoun invention under a pronoun-less roster** — full-strength here; the model genders 7 characters with zero roster authority. (Recurring corpus tell; this is a clean exemplar.)
2. **Invented buried-account owner** — "Diana = Princess D, who obtained the paternity test" crosses the boundary the calibration sessions hold; the name-pun + an "on-the-nose" director aside is treated as proof of ownership.
3. **Money mis-reconciliation propagating into the headline** — dropping Billy's +$50K bonus turned the true **$1.77M** into a headline-grade **"$1.71M."** A reconciliation bug is bad anywhere; here it's literally the title of the piece.
4. **Missed the highest-value character ingredient** — **sab001** (Sarah's declined $450K betrayal offer) never surfaces, leaving the widow thread (the corpus's most reliable source of a great thesis) the thinnest element of the report.
5. **Thesis-creep from cover-up to murder-conspiracy** — the disciplined "they bought the verdict" frame keeps sliding into "Victoria arranged his death," an indictment the ingredients don't license.

**Net:** a **promising-but-flawed early trace.** Right thesis-shape and a genuinely good structural instinct (Morgan as control), undercut by (a) a propagated $50K money error that mis-states the headline number, (b) one invented buried-owner, (c) wholesale pronoun invention, and (d) a missed widow ingredient that the strongest corpus reports would have made the spine. Old-name usage is era-consistent (faithful to the session roster), so it reads as a corpus-vintage marker rather than a drift failure. **Correct buried total: $1,770,000 / $1.77M, not $1.71M.**
