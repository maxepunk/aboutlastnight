## 5. Interaction-model contribution

**Success pattern (strong positive exemplar — the inverted-inheritance read).** This session adds the corpus's clearest case of the model finding the gap when the verdict scapegoats the *sympathetic* party and the corporate prize goes to *un-accused men*. The bespoke shape was set by three ingredient features all pointing the same way: (1) a verdict reverse-engineered from an act of **mercy** (the bathroom reconciliation → "collusion"); (2) a closer that **hands the company to Alex+Remi and merges the rivals** while warranting the two women; (3) a money ledger whose **#1 account buried exactly the Alex+Remi fraud trail** and whose chemist bought himself clean. The reusable success move is the deck's **"a much longer line"** framing plus the closer's **"that is the version that fits in a single sentence. The other version requires a chart"** — naming *why* the room scapegoated (narrative economy) rather than merely asserting it was wrong. The article also correctly **did not convict Alex/Remi of the murder** — it holds them to "make of that what you want," matching what the ingredients support (they inherited; cause of death stays open).

**Failure-signal it adds — the pronoun layer is the weak point.** Two pronoun errors (**Quinn he vs. doc "her"; Remi she vs. doc "two men/he"**) directly contradict the *only* pronoun signals the input doc supplies — and the Remi error is doubly bad because it collides with the article's own "executive team / both of them" beat that the closer leans on. This is the recurring "roster carries no explicit pronoun column, pipeline guesses, and guesses against the embedded-prose signal" tell. The lesson for the interaction model: when the roster line lacks pronouns, the parser must **harvest pronoun cues from the accusation/notes prose** ("her memory drugs," "two men… Remi, and Alex") and treat them as authoritative, rather than defaulting to a guess. Secondary soft edges: the "shell accounts" label on team handles, the slight "Taylor was buying it" overread, and a Taylor subplot that mildly out-weighs the harder succession gap.

**Net:** a high-quality, verdict-reading trace that nails the inverted-inheritance thesis and is exemplary on evidence-boundary, money, old-name, and meta hygiene — dragged down only by two source-contradicting pronoun assignments (Quinn, Remi) that a roster without a pronoun column failed to prevent.
