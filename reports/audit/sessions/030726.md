## 2. Latent best story (my ingredient-derived read, before the prose)

The verdict does two convenient things at once, and the gap is the story. "He killed himself" absolves everyone in the room; "but maybe Jess" exports the residual to an **absent, pregnant, sympathetic woman who cannot defend herself** and whose only "opportunity" is conjectural ("it would take no Herculean effort to noodge…"). Meanwhile the money says which secrets were worth millions to keep down. The **corruption/succession spine** — Riley's mapped shell vehicles (ril001/ril002), Mel's whole consolidated case (mel003, $450K), both Taylor recordings (tay004, $450K), Jamie's gossip — was **bought and buried**, overwhelmingly into **Cayman ($1.455M)**, while the public verdict settled on self-harm-plus-absent-girl. **The gap, then:** room's verdict = "Marcus did it to himself, or the absent mother did" → record = a room full of present beneficiaries (above all the **Vic→Alex succession** and the **Morgan shell-company machine**) plus a quiet, well-funded burial of exactly the corruption evidence that explains why Marcus was worth more dead. The compelling true story is **the convenience of the verdict and what the burial economy hid** — Jess is the perfect scapegoat *because* she's absent and the money points everywhere but at her. (Note: the ingredients do NOT support naming a killer; they support indicting the verdict's shape, the succession, and the buried corruption trail.)

## 5. Interaction-model contribution

This session's distinctive ingredient is a **doubly-evasive verdict** (self-inflicted **plus** absent-scapegoat) layered over a **director who all but hands over the counter-thesis** (the on-the-nose accounts engineered to frame Alex/Remi/Sarah; the pillow-cash "lead… into systemic corruption"). That mix produces the article's best instinct and its worst:

- **Success pattern (reinforced):** when the absent accused is a *sympathetic* figure (cf. 052326's coerced-chemist Quinn; 060526's framed widow), the cleanest true story is "the verdict is convenient *because* the accused can't answer, and the money points elsewhere." The article nails this with the single sharpest ingredient-grounded line in the corpus-to-date — *not a dollar of the $2.9M traces to Jess* — and threads it everywhere. Remote + guest-byline is again handled as a structural asset (Nova = wires, Ashe = floor), matching the 052326 calibration.
- **Failure-signal it adds (two, both new-ish):**
  1. **Atmospheric villain-invention at the close.** With no ingredient for an off-page power tier, the article conjures "the bosses at NeurAI / overlords / funders beyond Vic" and recasts Blake as their chessmaster. This is the inverse of the discipline the rest of the piece shows: where the money section *asks* who controls the accounts, the closing *answers* with an unsupported cabal. A note for the audit: counter-verdict ambition can curdle into invented conspiracy when the director's "continued investigation" hook is taken as license to name forces the record doesn't contain.
  2. **Roster-omitted pronouns → invented + contradicted.** With no pronoun column, the model back-filled and gendered **Ashe, Alex, and Vic against the director's own incidental gendering** — the clearest instance yet that a missing roster-pronoun field is an active fabrication risk, not a neutral gap.
- **Plus a clean data-integrity catch for the bundle layer:** the **financial-tracker "Total Buried $3,135,000" contradicts the body's "$2.9M"** by silently counting a **$225K admin adjustment** as buried money. The body got the number right; the widget did not — a template/aggregation bug where admin adjustments are summed into the burial total. Worth flagging system-wide (the Sarah-row + total both inherit the error).

**Bottom line:** the article found the most compelling true story latent in the material — the convenient verdict and the burial economy that points away from the absent accused — and built it from this session's exact ingredients, with disciplined evidence-boundary handling on the money. It is marred by (1) old-name drift ("Victoria"), (2) invented/contradicted pronouns for Ashe/Alex/Vic where the roster gave none, (3) a closing that invents an unsupported "bosses/overlords" tier, and (4) a financial-tracker total ($3.135M) that contradicts the body's correct $2.9M by folding in a $225K admin adjustment.
