Executive Summary

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Killed at his own party by the partner he'd been robbing for years. Alex Reeves, co-founder turned ghost, finally snapped after a decade of watching Marcus parade around in stolen glory. This investigation team recovered eighteen memory extractions that painted the complete picture: corporate theft, fraudulent AI technology, and a desperate man's last attempt to extract his way out of accountability. Alex is in custody. The hostages are safe. But the puppet master behind the memory extraction scheme? Still in the shadows.

Evidence Locker

The Stolen Empire: Memory extractions tell the same ugly story from multiple angles. Derek Thorne's recovered memory from June 2024 shows him discovering Marcus had stolen Alex's algorithm and claimed it as his own. Alex Reeves's extraction from 2022 confirms the original BizAI system was fundamentally broken until Alex rebuilt it from scratch—work Marcus happily took credit for. Leila Bishara's memory reveals Marcus lifted her proprietary indexing code from Oracle Ledger. The man built NeurAI on a foundation of other people's brilliance. Sofia Francisco's January 2027 extraction delivers the death blow: Marcus's "breakthrough AI" was fraudulent, requiring manual fixes for 87% of inputs. The emperor had no clothes, just better marketing.
The Breaking Point: Victoria Kingsley's memory extraction captures a strategic discussion about Marcus's mounting criminal liability—the board knew the walls were closing in. Jamie 'Volt' Woods's extraction reveals Victoria offering Marcus's job to Alex at the very party that would become his grave. Whether that was the final straw or just gasoline on an already-burning fire, Alex Reeves's 11:32 PM memory shows the violent assault—years of rage over stolen equity and uncredited work finally finding its target. Marcus went down hard.
The Extraction Scheme: Marcus Blackwood's own memory from 3:52 AM captures him executing a chaotic token-locking protocol on drugged partygoers. The man was harvesting memories from his own guests—corporate secrets, personal vulnerabilities, leverage. Leila Bishara's September 2026 extraction shows Marcus pressuring her for algorithm access while she was compromised. Sofia Francisco's memory of the $840 million AI training deal proves how valuable these extracted secrets could be. The party wasn't celebration—it was a heist.
The Casualties: Not everyone at that warehouse was a player in Marcus's games. Sarah Blackwood's extraction reveals she resigned from Facebook in 2020 to support her husband's startup—a sacrifice made for a con artist. Kai Andersen's memories show them being exploited for unpaid installation work, then publicly humiliated by Marcus. Ashe Motoko's extraction corroborates witnessing that cruelty firsthand. Howie Sullivan's 2:15 AM reflection captures a man watching Silicon Valley's corruption patterns play out yet again in real-time as idealism died on that dance floor.

Memory Analysis

Eighteen memory extractions formed the backbone of this investigation. The technology that Marcus weaponized against his guests became our primary evidence chain—poetic justice, if you believe in that sort of thing. Cross-referencing timestamps allowed us to reconstruct the night's timeline from multiple perspectives.

Critical memories that broke the case:

  • Alex Reeves, 11:32 PM: The assault itself—means, motive, opportunity captured in neural high-definition.
  • Marcus Blackwood, 3:52 AM: Proof of the extraction scheme, showing Marcus actively drugging and harvesting from guests.
  • Jamie Woods, party night: Victoria offering Alex the CEO position—establishing the boardroom conspiracy.
  • Derek Thorne, June 2024: First-hand witness to the original IP theft that started this whole tragedy.
  • Sofia Francisco, January 2027: Documentation that NeurAI's core technology was fraudulent.

Suspect Network

Alex Reeves — The ghost partner. Built the algorithm Marcus claimed as his own, watched his equity evaporate, his work credited to a thief. Currently in custody, awaiting formal charges. The assault is documented; whether murder sticks depends on forensics we haven't received.

Victoria Kingsley — Board power broker. Knew about Marcus's liabilities, was already negotiating succession. Her fingerprints are on the conspiracy, but she's lawyered up and untouchable—for now.

Sarah Blackwood — The wife who gave up everything for a man who was never what he claimed. Victim or accomplice? The memory of her sacrifice suggests the former.

Derek Thorne — Witnessed the original theft. His extraction proves he knew Marcus was dirty years ago. Why he stayed silent is a question for another day.

Outstanding Questions

  • Where is Marcus Blackwood's body? Blake arrived to find it missing—bodies don't walk themselves out.
  • What role did the "Black Market" play in the extraction technology's deployment?

Final Assessment

This investigation succeeded because five people woke up in an abandoned warehouse and chose to dig rather than run. Sarah Blackwood, Derek Thorn, Ashe Motoko, Victoria Kingsley, and James Whitman pieced together a decade of corporate betrayal from fragments of stolen memories while I led the raid on Old Canyon Road.

Alex Reeves had means, motive, and opportunity. The extractions prove it. But Alex was also a victim—a man systematically robbed by someone who called him partner. That doesn't excuse murder. It does explain it.

The bigger fish? Still swimming. Someone built the extraction infrastructure Marcus used. Someone taught him how to harvest memories from drugged partygoers. That someone is still out there, and they're watching us close this case like it's a loose end tied up neat.

It isn't. Not by a long shot.
