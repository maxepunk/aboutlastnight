## Closing rewrite (full)

**Current problems:** opener "In a world where extracted memories are a commodity" is generic (could paste into any session); doesn't develop the in-group/outsider thesis; gestures at the complacency-as-survival-strategy point without making it.

**Replacement:**

> The group accused Alex Reeves. The punch is real. The rage-text is real. The cease-and-desist is real. The case against Alex is the easiest case in the room to make, and this morning fifteen people made it.
>
> It is also the case that let everyone else look away from a memorial fund that isn't a charity, a VC overheard promising "He's out. You're in," a widow's lawyer opening a 12:50 AM meeting with "secret bank accounts," and a shell-company architect who took the smallest recorded cut of her own machine. The circle had spent the party at each other's throats — Sarah against Morgan, Mel against Vic, the Stanford Four against the crisis managers. It closed ranks the moment it had to choose between an insider and an outsider.
>
> Alex's January cease-and-desist didn't ask Marcus to pay. It asked the board to remove him and rebuild NeurAI around the code he'd stolen. She wasn't looking for a settlement — she was looking to take over. If she'd come back willing to sign an NDA and a check, the circle would have absorbed her. Instead she wanted the machine, and the machine is what the circle exists to protect. The punch gave them the pretext. They were always going to need one.
>
> Alex broke someone's nose. The police will be questioning her about a murder. Her cease-and-desist is effectively forgotten unless she clears her name. Mel is in Rome recovering from trauma. Sarah is taking the reins of NeurAI. The Marcus Memorial Fund took in $825,000 this morning. The laundering architecture outlived its architect, because the laundering architecture was never really his. It was theirs. It still is.

**Bridge test:** every systemic claim anchored (four specific session items in ¶2; specific C&D content in ¶3; specific figures + people in ¶4). Paste-into-another-session test: "The laundering architecture was never really his. It was theirs" could theoretically port to other sessions with shell-company systems, but is load-bearing here because the preceding sentence specifies the $825,000 and the architect. Pass.
