Marcus Blackwood Is Dead. His Memory Machine Is Just Getting Started.

Five people woke up with holes where their memories should be. Someone extracted those memories without permission. Someone locked them away. And someone made sure Marcus wouldn't be explaining why.

Nova | Independent Tech Accountability Journalist · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-0209-12

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Five people woke up in his warehouse with holes where their memories should be. Someone extracted those memories without permission. Someone locked them away. And someone made sure Marcus wouldn't be explaining why.

I was there when they started remembering what they'd lost. I watched Sarah Blackwood's face when she realized her marriage had ended twice that night. Once when Marcus betrayed her. Again when the memory of it was stolen from her head.

The group accused Marcus and Derek Thorne of distributing untested drugs that caused chaos at the party. They're right about the conspiracy. But I think they're wrong about the motive. The drugs weren't recreational. They were operational. Drugged people make terrible assistants. They make even worse witnesses.

The Story

Start with the affair. That's what broke Sarah Blackwood. Not the murder. Not the memory extraction. The betrayal.

Jessicah Kane was pregnant with Marcus's child. Sarah found out in a bathroom at 3:36 AM, mascara running, the other woman standing right there. I watched that memory play back on the screen. Two women who expected to be enemies discovering they were both victims of the same man.

The Bathroom Confession
JEK002 - 3:36AM - Bathroom. Both crying. SARAH knows. You know she knows. She says: 'The baby's his?' You nod. Expected rage. Get: 'You can have him. But that baby deserves better. We both do.' You're not enemies. You're both his victims.
Jessicah Kane

That's Jessicah's memory. Not Sarah's. Sarah can watch herself say those words, but she doesn't remember the feeling of saying them. That's what extraction does. It turns your own life into footage of someone else's.

But here's what I keep coming back to: why was Sarah staring at a wall, tears streaming, at 3:52 AM? She'd just found out about Jessicah. About the baby. And Marcus needed her to lock away extracted memories she couldn't even understand. The affair wasn't separate from the extraction. Sarah was a victim of both.

Now follow the drugs. July 4th, 2022. A backyard BBQ at Leila Bishara's place. Derek Thorne meets a fellow chemistry nerd named Oliver Sterling. A new compound. Fascinating side effects. The start of a wonderful friendship.

The Origin Story
DET001 - 04/07/2022 - LEILA's July 4 BBQ. You usually make your nut for the summer from this gig. She introduces you to OLIVER. Fellow chemistry nerd. Working on a new compound. Fascinating side effects. OLIVER is just excited someone is interested. This could be the start of a wonderful friendship.
Derek Thorne

Five years. That's how long it took to go from backyard curiosity to the drug administered at Marcus's party on February 21, 2027. Psychotrophin. Derek's formulation. Marcus's distribution network.

Derek knew things. Dangerous things. In a memory from June 2022, Marcus is high out of his mind, running his mouth about Alex Reeves's algorithm. The one Marcus claimed as his own. The one that built NeurAI.

The Algorithm Theft
DET002 - 06/09/2022 - God's favorite idiot. Jesus Christ, MARCUS is so high! He should not have told you that. The algorithm is NOT his! ALEX rewrote the whole thing and MARCUS somehow still gets the credit. You bite your tongue and take another hit. Note to self. REMAIN IRREPLACABLE.
Derek Thorne

Remain irreplaceable. That's Derek's survival strategy. He knew about the IP theft. He had the drug formulation Marcus needed. And according to texts I found, Derek knew something else: Victoria Kingsley faces jail because of Marcus. Derek had leverage on everyone.

So why was Victoria still investing? Twenty million dollars into NeurAI despite knowing about Alex's stolen algorithm. Despite the legal exposure. James Whitman pitched her AIBioComp and got rejected. Marcus got funded. James watched it happen and decided to do something about it.

July 2026. Marcus sees an opportunity. Everyone loves Derek's new drug. Someone wants to commercialize it. Go legit. Alex's tech will only take NeurAI so far. This offer might be the way to a unicorn.

The Commercialization Play
MAB002 - 07/15/2026 - A new opportunity. Everyone loves DEREK's new drug. And NOW, someone out there wants to commercialize the product. Go legit. ALEX's tech will take NeurAI only so far. THIS offer might be the way to a unicorn. Now… you just have to get DEREK to heel.
Marcus Blackwood

Get Derek to heel. That's how Marcus thought about his co-conspirator. A dog to be trained. A resource to be managed. No wonder Derek was upset that Marcus profited from his formulation.

But here's where all the threads converge. Marcus's own memory from 3:52 AM. The instructions were clear. Someone called 'THEY' wanted the extracted memories secured. Combinations only Marcus knew. A call when it was done.

The Safety Marcus Wouldn't Remove
MAB001 - ??/??/?? - MARCUS' memory extraction experiment notes. A heated argument. THEY want you to remove the safety. When the MEMORIES EXPIRE, they are RETURNED to their OWNERS. THEY would rather the memory is destroyed. You will NOT be doing that—thankyouverymuch. and THEY don't need to know.
Marcus Blackwood

Marcus built a safety into his technology. When memories expire, they return to their owners. THEY wanted that safety removed. THEY wanted memories destroyed permanently. Marcus refused. And THEY don't need to know he kept the safety in place.

Then 3:52 AM. Marcus trying to execute the plan. Drugged partygoers stumbling around his warehouse. Derek wandering off mid-command. Sarah staring at a wall. Chaos.

The System Failing
MAB003 - 3:52AM - The instructions were clear: "Make them secure the tokens. Combinations only you know. Call when done." Simple. The extractions worked. Now you just need these drugged zombies to LOCK them. "DEREK—take this token. Lock it the—." He wanders off mid-command. SARAH's staring at a wall, tears streaming. This is chaos. Don't think ANYONE has the right combinations anymore. Drugged people make terrible assistants. And THEY WILL be back if you don't call soon.
Marcus Blackwood

THEY will be back if you don't call soon. That's the last thing Marcus recorded before he died. Whoever THEY are, they were expecting a phone call. They got silence instead. And then Marcus was dead.

The drugs weren't about getting high. They were about compliance. You need people docile enough to have their memories extracted. Confused enough not to remember what happened. But Marcus miscalculated. Drugged people make terrible assistants. The chaos at the party wasn't a side effect. It was the system failing.

Follow the Money

Three shell accounts. Over $5 million buried. And every dollar traces back to someone who wanted the truth to stay hidden.

Anon2 is the monster. $3.72 million across a four-minute window between 9:04 and 9:08 PM. Whoever operates that account was buying silence at industrial scale. Sofia Francisco's memory alone cost $750,000 to bury. Someone really didn't want that one seeing daylight.

Then there's Anon. $1.175 million. Three transactions including two Victoria Kingsley memories back to back. $450,000 each. Whatever Victoria knew, someone decided it was worth nearly a million to keep quiet.

Sam is interesting. $475,000 across two transactions. The timing aligns suspiciously with bounty offers that came through the memory network. $50,000, then $150,000, offered to anyone willing to bury critical evidence. Black Market Holdings was recruiting. Someone took the job.

I watched James Whitman rush to the Valet with most of the group's memories. He shouted 'I WANT TO BURY ALL OF THESE.' The network went dark moments later. Victoria rejected his AIBioComp pitch. Marcus got her $20 million instead. Correlation or causation?

The money tells a story the evidence can't. Someone with deep pockets wanted this investigation to fail. They nearly succeeded.

The Players

I want to talk about Ashe Motoko for a second. They took meticulous notes throughout, systematically reconstructing events while everyone else was still reeling. Working closely with Alex Reeves. Personal and professional lines blurring. That's the kind of partnership that cracks cases.

Sarah Blackwood spent much of the investigation searching for a key to her purse. Inside: the same apology letter Marcus gave Jessicah. The affair confirmed twice over. She still brought evidence forward. That takes something I don't have a word for.

Victoria Kingsley assisted with locks and puzzles but kept a low profile. Helpful but invisible. I don't know what to make of that yet. Her memories are mostly buried. Nearly a million dollars worth. What was Victoria protecting?

"You can have him. But that baby deserves better. We both do."

— Sarah Blackwood, from Jessicah's extracted memory

Alex Reeves received offers from Black Market Holdings. The company that now controls NeurAI wanted him to take Marcus's old job in exchange for burying critical evidence. The algorithm Marcus stole was Alex's. The job was always supposed to be Alex's. But at what cost?

James Whitman rushed to bury evidence after Victoria rejected his pitch. The Anonymous 2 account shows coordinated activity. The network shutdown came moments after his outburst. I'm not making accusations. I'm noticing patterns.

Derek Thorne. The chemist. The leverage-holder. The man who knew about the IP theft and chose to remain irreplaceable. He had every reason to want Marcus gone. His formulation made the whole operation possible. And he was wandering off mid-command at 3:52 AM, too drugged to follow instructions.

What's Missing

Who is Oliver Sterling? Derek met him at a BBQ in 2022. A fellow chemistry nerd with a compound that had fascinating side effects. Five years later, that compound is administered at Marcus's party. Oliver's name appears in one memory. Then nothing. Where is he now?

Who are THEY? Marcus's memories reference someone giving him instructions. Make them secure the memories. Combinations only you know. Call when done. THEY wanted the safety removed so memories would be destroyed permanently. Marcus refused. Did that refusal cost him his life?

The whiteboard shows $99K connected to the affair. Rachel Torres found an undisclosed money source that Sarah needed to discuss urgently. What was that money for? Silence? Support? The trail goes cold.

Victoria invested $20 million despite knowing about the IP theft. Derek texted that she faces jail because of Marcus. Why would she take that risk? What did she know that made the investment worth potential prison?

Five people woke up that morning. The other partygoers vanished along with Marcus's body. No one can account for them. No one knows where they went. The silence has a shape. The shape has a price tag. This one cost $5.37 million.

The group accused Marcus and Derek of distributing untested drugs. The evidence supports the conspiracy. But the chaos was the cover story. Underneath it: extracted memories, buried evidence, corporate succession, and instructions from someone Marcus called THEY.

Derek and Marcus may have been guilty. But they weren't alone.

Here's what I keep thinking about: five people woke up with their memories stolen. Not metaphorically. Actually extracted. And the first thing that happened? Someone started buying those memories. Paying for silence. Turning human experience into a transaction.

That's not a murder mystery. That's a business model. First they wanted your clicks. Then your conversations. Now they want what it felt like to be you.

I was in that room. I watched them piece it together. And I'm telling you: whoever THEY are, they're still out there. And they have $5.37 million worth of memories that belong to someone else.
