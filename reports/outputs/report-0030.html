The Party That Stole Your Mind

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Most of his guests are missing. And someone paid $4.1 million to make sure no one remembers why.

Nova | Senior Investigative Reporter · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-0213-23

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Five people woke up the morning after his party. The rest? Nobody knows where they went. But I know what happened to their memories. Someone extracted them. Someone drugged them first. And someone paid over four million dollars to make sure the evidence stayed buried.

I was there when the survivors started piecing together what they'd lost. Watching them play back memories they couldn't actually remember living. That's the thing about extracted memories. You can see yourself on the screen, hear your own voice, and feel absolutely nothing. Because the experience isn't yours anymore. It belongs to whoever took it.

This is what I found.

The Story

Five survivors. That's what we started with. Five people who woke up in that warehouse with gaps where the night should have been. The rest of the guest list? Vanished. No bodies. No witnesses. Just absence shaped like people who used to be there.

Cassandra's sources found no evidence of Marcus's body in the warehouse building. No evidence of the murder occurring there at all. Just his secret workshop, hidden behind a wall that shouldn't have existed, filled with equipment that shouldn't have been legal.

The missing partygoers left something behind, though. Their memories. Extracted without consent, locked in containers they'll never open. Someone has those memories now. Someone is keeping them.

Sarah Blackwood spent the early part of her night searching for a key. Just a key to her purse. She didn't know what she'd find when she opened it. Marcus's apology letter to Jessicah Kane. Proof of an affair she'd suspected but never confirmed. The baby Jessicah was carrying? Marcus's.

At 9:20 PM, Jessicah tried to talk to Marcus about the baby. He blew her off. All she wanted was two minutes. Howie Sullivan overheard, offered her water and kind words. The last thing she needed.

The Reconciliation
JEK002 - 3:36AM - Bathroom. Both crying. SARAH knows. You know she knows. She says: 'The baby's his?' You nod. Expected rage. Get: 'You can have him. But that baby deserves better. We both do.' You're not enemies. You're both his victims.
Jessicah Kane

By 3:36 AM, Sarah and Jessicah found each other in a bathroom. Both crying. Sarah said it plainly: the baby deserves better. They both did. For one moment, they weren't rivals. They were both Marcus's victims. And then morning came, and Jessicah was gone with the others.

That reconciliation happened. I watched it play back on the screen. But neither of them remembers it. The forgiveness that took all night to reach? Extracted. Gone. Jessicah vanished before she could know that Sarah had let go of the rage.

Here's what connected the missing to the survivors: Derek Thorne's drug. Psychotrophin3B. He didn't ask anymore, just offered. I watched Flip's memory play back. 10:00 PM. Derek catches Flip's eye. The drugs help Flip smile through Rachel Torres laughing at jokes about contractors. The edge smooths. That's what Derek was selling. Smooth edges. Compliance.

The Dealer's Routine
FLI004 - 10:00PM - DEREK doesn't ask anymore, just offers. The edge smooths. RACHEL laughs at your venue acoustics joke. She mentions respecting 'people who work with their hands'—artisans, she means. Not contractors. Not you. DEREK catches your eye. The drugs help you smile.
Flip

Derek formulated it. Marcus saw dollar signs. In July 2026, Marcus was already planning the pivot. Alex Reeves's algorithm would only take NeurAI so far. But Derek's drug? Someone out there wanted to commercialize it. Go legit. Marcus called it the way to a unicorn.

There was just one problem. Derek wasn't the type to heel.

The Unicorn Dream
MAB002 - 07/15/2026 - A new opportunity. Everyone loves DEREK's new drug. And NOW, someone out there wants to commercialize the product. Go legit. ALEX's tech will take NeurAI only so far. THIS offer might be the way to a unicorn. Now… you just have to get DEREK to heel.
Marcus Blackwood

But Derek wasn't at the party. Not on the guest list. The architect of the chaos, absent from his own crime scene. Someone else was distributing. Someone else was making sure the drugs hit the right people at the right time. Derek orchestrated from a distance while others took the fall.

By 3:52 AM, the chaos was complete. I watched Marcus's own memory play back, and what I saw turned my stomach. The instructions were clear: make them secure the tokens. Combinations only he knew. Call when done. Simple. The extractions had worked. Now he just needed these drugged zombies to lock them.

Derek wandered off mid-command. Sarah was staring at a wall, tears streaming. Marcus was trying to run an operation with people who couldn't remember their own names. Drugged people make terrible assistants. And THEY would be back if he didn't call soon.

Marcus Blackwood

THEY. That's the word that stopped me cold. Marcus was taking instructions. The man who built NeurAI, who stole Alex's algorithm, who turned human memory into a product line, was answering to someone else. And THEY would be back.

Who was Marcus calling? Who are THEY? The memory cuts off before those questions get answered.

But I found something else. Marcus's experiment notes. Undated. A heated argument. THEY wanted him to remove the safety feature. When the memories expired, they were supposed to return to their owners. THEY wanted them destroyed instead. Marcus refused that one. Small mercy. And THEY didn't need to know he was keeping that safety in place.

The Safety Feature
MAB001 - ??/??/?? - MARCUS' memory extraction experiment notes. A heated argument. THEY want you to remove the safety. When the MEMORIES EXPIRE, they are RETURNED to their OWNERS. THEY would rather the memory is destroyed. You will NOT be doing that—thankyouverymuch. and THEY don't need to know.
Marcus Blackwood

So Marcus had a line he wouldn't cross. He'd steal your memories, drug you into compliance, and hand you over to anonymous partners. But he wouldn't destroy those memories forever. He kept one safety feature in place, and he kept it secret from his handlers.

That might be why he's dead.

At 2:02 AM that night, Jamie Woods was watching the powerful play their games. Marcus handed a fat envelope to a DC type who'd been hanging around lately. Loaded with Franklins. And this is the same Marcus who asked Jamie to go easy on the top shelf drinks. Penny-wise. Bribing officials while pinching pennies on the bar tab.

The Bribe
JAV021 - 02:02AM- Untouchable. These motherfuckers think they run the world. Look at MARCUS and that DC type that's been hanging around lately. MARCUS just handed over a fig fat envelope. You know that's loaded full of Franklins. HA! And MARCUS asked you to go easy on the top shelf drinks tonight. Penny-wise. Fucking Idiot..
Jamie "Volt" Woods

Black Market Holdings. That's who controls NeurAI now. They own the shares. They have the technology. And they made Alex Reeves an offer he should have refused.

They offered him Marcus's old job. The man whose algorithm Marcus stole, now being asked to take the dead man's chair. In exchange for burying evidence that would implicate NeurAI.

I watched Alex work alongside Ashe Motoko for hours, methodically reconstructing events, devoted to uncovering the truth. And then Black Market Holdings came calling with a job offer and a price for silence. $150,000 transmitted through the memory network. Another $50,000 before that.

Follow the Money

Three shell accounts. Over four million dollars buried. And every dollar traces back to someone in that room who decided silence was worth more than truth.

Anon2 is the largest account by far. $3.545 million across more than fifteen transactions, almost all of them in an eight-minute window between 9:06 and 9:08 PM. That's not individual decisions. That's coordinated suppression. Someone was running an operation.

The timing tells its own story. The first transaction hit Anon at 8:58 PM. $450,000. Then another $450,000 a minute later. By 9:01, Sam received $250,000. And then Anon2 exploded. Transaction after transaction, $10,000 here, $750,000 there, all flooding into one account in a panicked rush.

James Whitman rushed to Black Market Holdings shouting he wanted to bury everything. I saw him do it. Whatever was in those memories, he didn't want anyone else to see. The timing of his outburst correlates uncomfortably with the Anon2 surge.

Victoria Kingsley helped with locks and puzzles throughout the night. Quiet. Unobtrusive. Anonymous, even. That's a curious choice for someone with $163 million at stake. Skyler Iyer had warned her about the Alex-Marcus conflict. She knew what was at risk. And she stayed invisible.

Sam. Two transactions. $475,000 total. The timing correlates with Alex being approached about Marcus's job. The second offer of $150,000 hit the network minutes after a $250,000 transaction to Sam. Make of that what you will.

The Players

Ashe Motoko became the investigation's backbone. I watched her take meticulous notes, acting as group scribe and analyst, systematically working through every unlocked memory to reconstruct what happened. When I needed to understand the timeline, I looked at Ashe's work. Her BizAI privacy investigation had already flagged Marcus's memory experiments months ago. When Black Market Holdings took control, her thoroughness became a threat.

Alex Reeves worked alongside Ashe for hours, devoted to uncovering the truth about the man who stole his life's work. The cease and desist letter is on record. Alex suing Marcus over the algorithm. The algorithm that wasn't Marcus's at all. Derek's memory from June 2022 proves it: Marcus was so high he admitted the whole thing. Alex rewrote everything. Marcus took the credit.

"You can have him. But that baby deserves better. We both do."

— Sarah Blackwood to Jessicah Kane, 3:36 AM

Sarah Blackwood came looking for a key and found her husband's betrayal. But what she did with that discovery matters more than how she found it. She found Jessicah. They talked. They cried together. And Sarah chose grace over rage. Both his victims, she said. Not enemies.

Then Jessicah vanished with the others. The reconciliation Sarah fought so hard to reach? Gone before morning. The forgiveness exists only as extracted memory now, playing back for strangers who weren't there.

Victoria Kingsley stayed largely anonymous. Helped with locks. Helped with puzzles. Never drew attention. I'm not sure what to make of that. Someone with $163 million on the line usually has opinions. Victoria's opinions stayed buried.

James Whitman made his opinions very clear. He rushed to Black Market Holdings shouting his intentions to bury everything. The four witnesses to his outburst had been given dangerous doses of experimental mind-altering substances. Their credibility is compromised. But I saw him. I watched him run.

What's Missing

The missing partygoers haunt this story. Only five people woke up. The rest simply weren't there anymore. Not dead, as far as we know. Just absent. Cassandra's sources found no evidence of Marcus's body in the warehouse. No bodies at all. Just equipment that shouldn't exist and silence that cost millions.

Derek formulated Psychotrophin3B. Derek distributed it through the party. But Derek wasn't on the guest list that night. Who were his hands on the ground? Someone was passing out those smooth edges. Someone was making sure the drugs hit the right people.

The instructions at 3:52 AM were clear. Make them secure the tokens. Call when done. But who was Marcus calling? Who are THEY? The memory cuts off before we get names. Before we get faces. Before we understand who was pulling Marcus's strings.

Jessicah and Sarah reconciled at 3:36 AM. By morning, Jessicah was gone. Did someone decide that reconciliation was too dangerous to exist? Did someone watch those two women find peace and decide it needed to be erased?

Black Market Holdings controls NeurAI now. They offered Alex the job Marcus held. Why would the new owners want the man who sued Marcus? Unless they wanted to buy his silence. Unless they knew what he could expose.

The Anon2 account holder paid $3.545 million to keep something quiet. I can tell you the shape of that silence. I can tell you it happened in eight frantic minutes. I cannot tell you what's inside it. Someone can. Someone knows exactly what was worth that much to bury.

The group accused Marcus and Derek. Derek as architect, Marcus as distributor. The evidence supports it. Derek's drug. Marcus's distribution network. The chaos that made extraction possible. The compliance that made it easy.

But the accusation misses the larger architecture. Someone gave Marcus instructions. Someone funded the burial. Someone made the witnesses disappear. Marcus and Derek may be guilty. They're not the only ones.

This isn't just a murder. It's a proof of concept. Drug the witnesses. Extract their memories. Pay for their silence. Make the inconvenient ones disappear. First they wanted your clicks. Then your data. Now they want what it felt like to be you. And they'll pay to make sure you never remember.

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Derek is in the wind. And somewhere, THEY are still waiting for their call.
