- Story ¶5 ("leaning on a chemist, humiliating an artist, dodging a journalist...") — the rapid-fire list is deliberately monotone, and earns it.

**Editorial hedges (CUT — these are weakness):**

- **"That's a sentence that can mean anything."** — Players, Remi paragraph. Before "In this room, this morning, it mostly meant she was talking to the person with the checkbook." — the first sentence hedges what the second one commits to. - **"A man genuinely disoriented by an evening others were orchestrating around him, or a man performing disorientation."** — Players ¶6. Valid boundary hedge (Nova can't know which). KEEP.

- **"But even though Alex made a compelling argument..."** — Players ¶6. "Compelling argument" without sourcing = editorial puff. Cut the evaluation; report the argument.

No major editorial-hedge infestation. Just a few softening phrases.
