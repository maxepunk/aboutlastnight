These are not hard violations — the article uses softening language ("Make of that what you will," "reads as a… dry joke," "possibly an architect") — but they walk the line and one of them is worth tightening.

### C1. Name-pattern decoding: Saarinen → Flip (BORDERLINE)

**Follow the Money (line 1638):** "Saarinen, named for the architect, spread $1,055,000 across six transactions. I overheard Flip telling someone he was a 'Party Architect.' Make of that what you will."

**Rule:** "Speculate from behavior, not names or mappings. When theorizing about anonymous accounts, use director-observed patterns (who was seen near Blake…)." The Saarinen→Flip insinuation rests on decoding the account name (Eero Saarinen = architect) + an overheard self-descriptor, not on director-observed burial activity. Director noted Flip was "everywhere and nowhere at once" — explicitly NOT placed at Blake's booth.

**Verdict:** Technically softened enough with "Make of that what you will" that it's a reader-drawn inference. But purists might call it out. If we keep it, lean harder on the "I overheard" observation and less on the name decoding.

### C2. Name-pattern decoding: Party Guy → Quinn (BORDERLINE)

**Follow the Money (line 1646):** "Party Guy, $980,000 across four transactions, **reads as a pharmacologist's dry joke.**"

Same pattern — decoding "Party Guy" as pointing at the chemist who supplies the drugs. Softened with "reads as" framing. **Verdict:** Borderline. The softening works but it's still name-decoding. Consider replacing with a director-observed behavior beat (Quinn's box negotiation with Nat and Mel is a gift — Quinn was visibly transacting in tokens).

### C3. "funded by burying memories of him" (re: Marcus Memorial Fund)

**Follow the Money (line 1642):** "A fund named for the dead man, **funded by burying memories of him**…"

### C4. "Alex… named stake in the ledger" / "Morgan… took the smallest cut"

**Follow the Money (line 1646):** "Alex: $150,000, one transaction. The woman the group accused has the smallest named stake in the ledger aside from Morgan. Morgan: $75,000, one transaction. The architect of the shell company infrastructure **took the smallest cut** of all."
