NEURAI INVESTIGATION

The Story They Could All Agree On

Twelve people agreed on Vic Kingsley in the commotion minutes before the police arrived. By morning the accused was on a plane to the Cayman Islands. Five shell accounts were $2.375 million richer for the silences nobody voted on.

Cassandra Nova | Senior Investigative Reporter · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-050826-26

Minutes before the police were called, a source on the ground described the scene to me in a single image: twelve investigators clustered together, arguing loudly, deciding what version of the night to hand over. By the time the sirens reached them they had picked one. Nine votes for Vic Kingsley. Two for Alex Reeves. One each for Sarah Blackwood and Jess. By morning Vic's office confirmed he had taken a vacation to the Cayman Islands to recover from the trauma of last night's events. The Caymans are non-extradition by treaty. That is the kind of detail competent exit counsel would have flagged before he booked the flight.

Twelve people, one dead founder, forty-eight extracted memories on the public record by morning. They exposed nearly everything they found.

Nearly. Five shell accounts moved $2.375 million out of that room anonymously. Someone collected a $50,000 premium for being the first to walk up to Blake. That premium was paid before any consensus existed. This is the story inside the story my source sent me.

THE STORY

Vic arrived with a plan. His own extracted memory from 9:55PM documents it in his words: Alex in, Marcus out, no more cease and desist letters, no more potential criminal conspiracy charges, NeurAI preserved as a viable investment. By 11:35PM the plan was operational on the sidewalk. Jamie was outside trying to have a cigarette in peace when the wind carried her a sentence she did not ask for.

Bartender's Sidewalk Earful — 11:39PM
The dude who punched MARCUS is out here with that VC. They're in a panic. Meeting outside, trying to be discreet, but the wind carries this sentence: 'He's out. You're in. Trust me. It's done. Please just come inside.' There's some intense back and forth. You hear the name SARAH. Then they finally convince the punching man to head back inside.
Jamie

Alex had punched Marcus minutes earlier. Vic was telling him, on a sidewalk he did not control, that the chair was already his. By 1:08AM Alex had a different message. Remi had handed him a laptop. Alex ran the comparison. He walked Vic and Remi through what the comparison showed.

The Sham Revealed — 1:08AM
You hand the laptop back to REMI. NeurAI's demo ran beautifully and made big claims. But the code behind it doesn't match up. You estimate that MARCUS's 'breakthrough AI' needs manual fixes for 90% of the inputs. You show VIC the specs. Their $30 million dollar investment? It bought them a sham. MARCUS's code is worthless.
Alex

The room would give Vic nine votes. The whiteboard would write THE MARCUS PROBLEM under his name with a 9 next to it. What the whiteboard did not record is what Vic learned at 1:08AM. The thirty million dollar investment he had spent his coup protecting had bought a sham. The accusation found a man who had already lost the thing he committed the crime to keep.

Taylor was at 9:51PM the journalist who had gotten Ashe fired. The mechanism was simple. Taylor had tipped Marcus to Ashe's expose before it ran. Marcus pulled strings. The expose was buried. Ashe lost her job at the Silicon Valley Business Journal. Taylor got the NeurAI launch exclusive.

The Drunk Brag — 9:51PM
TAYLOR: 'I can't believe that ASHE is even showing their face here. What a loser. I mean, look. They had the receipts on MARCUS. But they never understood that the story's not about the truth. No sirree… I know you gotta keep some doors open. Don't get me wrong. Sucks to be ASHE - grinding away at their blog or whatever, then showing up here as some loser's plus one… It makes me appreciate my spot on the VIP list. Gotta be grateful for the small things, you know? And I did get the exclusive on NeurAI's launch out of it.'
Taylor

The story is not about the truth. That was Taylor at the start of the evening. By 12:40AM, the calculation had inverted. Morgan, who had spent months collecting documentation on Marcus's shell companies and lobbying conflicts and insider schemes, walked up to Taylor's recorder. He had been holding everything in reserve. He stopped holding.

Morgan Goes On Record — 12:40AM
You would have told MARCUS to his face that you weren't going to play ball anymore, if he'd given you the chance. Instead, he bluffed. And you're calling. TAYLOR turns on their recorder and you begin to spill the worst things you have on MARCUS. It's a long list.
Morgan

By the time the room gathered to vote, Taylor was quick to turn on Vic, the partner she had worked alongside throughout the night. My source flagged Taylor and Ashe as the night's two most crucial contributors to the public record. Both of them are journalists. Their token submissions were anonymous, like everyone else's, but the room could not have produced what it produced without them.

At 11:22PM, Morgan was on a hammock at the side of the house with Marcus. The two of them were alone there, and Morgan brought what he had spent months gathering.

The Hammock Ultimatum — 11:22PM
You're both on the hammock. You show him proof: bribes, fraudulent reports, shell company records, stolen IP... You say: 'Go public about the insider trading and take full responsibility or I give all of this damning evidence to Senator Walsh.' His face says he's considering it, but there's something else there. Instead of answering, he reaches into his bag and hands you a generous bribe. MARCUS: 'Take care of it, will you? Just like you always do.'
Morgan

Seven minutes later, the pattern had a second witness.

Flip Sees the Envelope — 11:29PM
Untouchable. These motherfuckers think they run the world. Look at MARCUS and that DC type he's been hanging around with lately. MARCUS just casually handed over a fat wad of cash like it's nothing to him. And he gave YOU such a hard time about the money he loaned you tonight…
Flip

That is the most evidentially solid moment in the file. The room's whiteboard listed Morgan with a single annotation under his name: BLACKMAILED BY MARCUS. The framing was Morgan as victim, not active operator. Morgan received zero votes. The strongest corroborated evidence in the file was for a crime the room did not address.

There was a deeper crime running underneath all of this. In October 2024, an unidentified intruder broke into Ezra Sullivan's office at the Electronic Frontier Foundation and took files from the 2020 incubator program. The police report has no named suspect. Inside that program was Quinn's old work on Psychotrophin-3A. Quinn's own recorded memory asks the question directly: how did Marcus find out? The fact that he surfaced months later with detailed knowledge of the drug, blackmailing Quinn into resuming research they had buried for safety reasons, is not the kind of coincidence that survives close reading. Sam Thorne's diary entries, recovered as physical evidence, describe him manufacturing "the shit I'm making for him." There was a supply chain with months of lead time, and at the top of it was a coercion.

Quinn Under Blackmail — 9:59PM
Somehow, MARCUS found out about the unusual side effects of Psychotrophin3A, and is obsessed with its ability to induce neural plasticity in large doses. Why did you bury the PT-3A safety assessments? Now he's got you by the balls and is blackmailing you to continue with your experiments to develop it. This drug is dangerous, and should not be in the wrong hands.
Quinn

The drug was used on people at the party. At 11:04PM Jess told her friend Nat what that had been like. The recording from 11:04PM is the only on-record account from someone who went through the extraction protocol and survived to describe it.

The Extraction Protocol — Jess's Account — 11:04PM
You approach your friend NAT and tell them you know why MARCUS is interested in Psychotrophin-3A. He uses it as part of his 'Memory Extraction Protocol.' Said it was an experimental NeurAI research he wasn't ready to go public with. You know about it because he tried it on you once. Said it was an acting gig, that you should play a butler. You wanted to help him, so you took the drugs and let him run his experiment. He was ELATED. Showed you a token. Said your memory was stored on it somehow.
Jess

At 12:32AM, while Jamie froze behind the bar trying to be invisible, a stranger walked into Marcus's party space and made the demand that explains everything that came after.

Skyler's Demand — 12:32AM
'You missed our deadline.' MARCUS goes pale. 'Tonight we were supposed to do a test run. But you say you're AI's still buggy. So let's try programming my prototype with your memory recordings instead. What's the worst that could happen?' MARCUS: 'SKYLER, it's untested technology. What if something goes wrong...' No response.
Jamie

The five most consequential items in the entire evidence file are concentrated in this thread. Marcus's own extracted memory at 1:50AM is self-incriminating about the extractions he ran on people at the party. Sam manufactured the drug. Quinn was coerced into supplying it. Skyler walked in and demanded that the prototype be loaded with the results. The whiteboard listed Quinn and Skyler by name. The vote went to Vic.

Marcus got Jess pregnant. He cleaned out the joint bank account before Sarah knew the marriage was ending. Then he sent both women apology cards. The same printed rose on the front. The same line inside. I fucked up. I'm sorry. She means nothing to me. Different initials. Different riddles for where the gift was hidden. That is the document trail of a serial template.

Riley had spent months strategically befriending Morgan to gather intelligence on Marcus's shell companies. Not for herself. For Mel, who was Sarah's attorney. The investigation into Marcus's betrayal of Sarah was already months deep before the party started.

At 1:36AM, Sarah found Jess in the bathroom. The article's strongest human moment is hers.

The Bathroom — 1:36AM
You find JESS in the bathroom, crying, and suddenly put it together. You squeak out between unexpected tears: 'MARCUS's baby is yours?' She's sobbing so hard now that she can't even speak, so she nods. You thought you'd be outraged, but seeing her here in front of you, you just feel resigned. You say: 'You can have him. But that baby deserves better. We both do.'
Sarah

The arcs above are what the room saw clearly. What the room chose to do with that clarity costs $2.375 million to explain.

FOLLOW THE MONEY

Forty-eight extracted memories went to the public record that night. Seven went to Blake's Black Market. Five shell accounts collected the proceeds. None of the names on those accounts match anyone in the room. The Kai account deserves a footnote. Kai Andersen, the installer whose own extracted memories appear in the public record, was not present at the investigation. Someone in that room used a name that wasn't theirs.

Brittany is the number that matters most. The base figure was $150,000. The total is $200,000. The $50,000 difference is the premium Blake offered to whoever was first to walk up. Before any consensus existed, before anyone had voted, before the room had even agreed there was a story to negotiate, someone in that room made a calculation and moved fastest. The forty-eight-to-seven ratio looks like radical transparency. The Brittany bonus reveals the room's first reflex.

THE PLAYERS

Two of the twelve made themselves visible in ways my source flagged as crucial. Ashe and Taylor are both journalists. Their individual token submissions were anonymous, like everyone else's. What they did instead was harder to render anonymous. Ashe walked into Taylor's 9:51PM drunk brag and confronted her on the spot. She had already lost her job at the Silicon Valley Business Journal because Taylor had leaked her expose to Marcus. By 12:42AM Ashe was sitting across from Ezra Sullivan with a tape recorder, conducting an on-record interview Ezra had not granted to anyone in forty years.

The others who shaped the investigation did so without signing. Riley spent months befriending Morgan to build the intelligence file Mel would need as Sarah's attorney. Sam handed his laptop to Remi at 11:17PM. That handoff is the chain that produced the code comparison at 1:08AM. Mel had the Stanford Four emergency meeting called by 12:50AM. Sam and Jess were flagged by my source as the investigation's most consistent collaborators. Most of these people exposed evidence. Most of them stayed anonymous doing it.

The shell accounts above moved $2.375 million through ten anonymous signatures. My source could not confirm who operated them. That is what the design is for.

WHAT'S MISSING

Seven extracted memories went to Blake's Black Market under names that match no one in the room. The totals are listed above. What I cannot tell you is whose memories funded which account, or what was inside them. What I can tell you is that someone paid the $50,000 premium to be first to walk up. Before the deliberation started, someone in that room had already decided what was worth converting.

The Kai account is the cleanest illustration of how this works. Kai Andersen, the installer, was not at the investigation. Someone used the name anyway. The account is a borrowed identity, confirmed absent. Whoever directed funds to Kai signed someone else's name. I cannot tell you who did that. I can tell you it is not a coincidence that the name they chose belonged to the installer who was in the exposed evidence but not in the room.

Seven choices, out of fifty-five possible. That specificity is information. What kind of memory tries hardest to disappear? What questions does $2.375 million try to make unanswerable? My source described the commotion among the investigators minutes before the police were called: they were loudly debating their findings and the story that should be passed along to the police. I cannot reconstruct that debate. I can tell you the buried accounts were already funded by then.

CLOSING

The harder question is what the vote did not address. The memory extraction protocol Marcus was running on people at the party without their consent. Jess described what that was like to her friend Nat at 11:04PM, and her account is the only first-person testimony we have from someone who survived it. The Psychotrophin-3A supply chain Marcus coerced Quinn into supplying with knowledge of research that had vanished from their former mentor's office months earlier. Skyler's demand at 12:32AM that the prototype be loaded with the stolen recordings. None of that received a vote. The whiteboard listed Quinn and Skyler. The room voted nine times for Vic.

Skyler's question, what's the worst that could happen, is not rhetorical. It is the industry's current operating philosophy. When the data extraction business runs out of clicks and conversations, it reaches for something more literal. Marcus did not invent the demand. Skyler did. Quinn did not volunteer the supply. Marcus blackmailed it out of them. Sam manufactured what was demanded. The system that killed Marcus Blackwood did not die with him. It was in the room. It is still funding prototypes.

The twelve investigators voted their consensus, debated their story, and walked out. Most of them exposed everything they found. That matters. But in a room where the commotion happened before the police arrived, and where five shell accounts were already funded before the vote began, the question I cannot answer is whose silences the consensus protected. The Caymans don't answer it either. The other question is who at NeurAI gains, with both Marcus and Vic now subtracted from the board.
