## Findings (priority order)

### CE1 — [HIGH / structural] The Players collapses into two dense run-on paragraphs
This is the one real structural problem. Two issues stacked:

**(a) The solidarity beat gets buried.** Paragraph 2 opens by closing the Jess–Sarah beat — "Two memories describe that bathroom… two of Marcus's victims, recognizing each other" — and then, *in the same paragraph*, immediately pivots to a character roundup ("Now consider the rest…"). The most thematically important line in the section (the victims recognizing each other) doesn't get to land before the subject changes.

**(b) The roundup is monotone and overstuffed.** Five characters (Ashe, Taylor-as-foil, Alex, then Remi, Riley, Mel) are crammed into two long paragraphs, each at the same flat tempo. The Mel and Remi entries are especially dense after Pass 1's citations.

> **Fix (story-level, not sentence-level):**
> - Let "…two of Marcus's victims, recognizing each other." **end its own paragraph.** Give the solidarity its beat.
> - Make the roundup's through-line **explicit and load-bearing**: the room was full of people with motive — Ashe (career destroyed), Alex (stolen company), Remi (frozen out), Riley (protecting Sarah), Mel (undisclosed conflict) — *and it named the one with the least power.* That sentence is the section's thesis; right now it's implied, not stated.
> - Break the wall of names into shorter units (per character or logical pairing) so each gets air. Don't add length — redistribute it.

### CE2 — [MEDIUM / redundancy — introduced by Pass 1] Alex's cease-and-desist now appears twice
Pass 1 added the full C&D detail to **The Players** ("her cease-and-desist letter… demanded Marcus be removed as CEO and replaced by a neutral interim chief, and gave the board until the end of the month to comply"). The **Closing** already carried it ("Alex's cease and desist demanded Marcus's removal and a neutral interim CEO in his place"). Same fact, two full statements.
> **Fix:** The C&D's "remove Marcus / install a neutral interim CEO" detail is most load-bearing in the **Closing**, where it pays off "the chair defaulted to Sarah." Keep it there. In **The Players**, reduce to the parts that serve that section's job — Alex had a live IP claim with a deadline a week out, and her name hangs over an account she was never near — and drop the "neutral interim CEO" clause (let the Closing own it). First mention can simply name the document ("her cease-and-desist letter, filed through Patchwork Law") without re-stating its demands in full.

### CE3 — [MEDIUM / clarity] "the rest of the people who were scoring no one when it counted"
The Players, P2. "Scoring no one" is opaque game-economy phrasing — unclear whether it means voting, accusing, or burying. Abstract where the rest of the article is concrete.
> **Fix:** Say what's meant: the others in the room who had their own reasons to want Marcus gone. Tie it to the through-line in CE1.

### CE4 — [LOW / throat-clearing] "So let me put on the record what the two of them actually did…"
The Players, P1. Mild wind-up. It's close to Nova's voice (she's a reporter setting the record straight), so it's defensible — but "So let me put on the record" + "because the room had it in front of them and voted against it" is a touch throat-clear-y.
> **Fix (optional):** trim to a tighter version, e.g., "Here is what the two of them actually did — and what the room had in front of it when it voted against them."

### CE5 — [LOW / redundancy] Double can/can't antithesis in What's Missing
P2 runs two parallel boundary antitheses back to back: "I can see what those memories cost. I cannot see what they said." … "I can tell you the shape of the silence. I can't tell you what's inside it." Both are good; together they're slightly repetitive.
> **Fix:** keep one (the stronger is "I can see what those memories cost. I cannot see what they said."), fold the other's idea in, or cut it. Preserves the boundary point with more punch.

### CE6 — [LOW / polish] Lede P2 dangling clause
"…from a leak that reached me while I was writing this about who will run his company now." The "about who will run his company now" dangles awkwardly off "writing this."
> **Fix:** "…and from a leak that reached me, as I wrote, about who will run his company now." (or similar reorder).

### CE7 — [LOW / optional] Photo 8 placement is thematically loose
The Story's inline photo 8 (Riley + Ashe forcing the workshop door) sits between the Marcus-character paragraph and the Jess paragraph; neither mentions the locked room, so the image floats as generic investigation texture. Not wrong (palate image), and it does feature two roster members. Leave unless we want a tighter image-text link.
