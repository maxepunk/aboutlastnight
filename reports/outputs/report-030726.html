The Inheritance That Wasn't There Yet

Marcus Blackwood is dead. His unborn child stands to inherit millions. The group accused the mother. But the money trails lead everywhere except to her.

Cassandra Nova | Senior Investigative Reporter · Ashe Motoko | Guest Reporter · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-0309-04

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Somewhere out there, a woman is carrying his child. And last night, a group of people gathered to investigate his murder decided she killed him for the inheritance. But the live tips flooding my inbox tell a different story. Seven people woke up after that party with holes where their memories should be. Someone extracted those memories without permission. Someone locked them away. And now someone is making sure the money trails lead everywhere except to the truth.

A paternity test confirms it: Marcus Blackwood, 99.85% biological father. The math is clean. The motive is clean. A pregnant ex-lover, abandoned, with a child who stands to inherit a fortune. Jessicah Kane. That's who the group accused. But Jessicah wasn't there for the investigation. She ran before it started. And the $2.9 million in buried secrets? Not a single dollar traces back to her.

The Night of the Party

The extracted memories tell us what happened at that warehouse party. The night Marcus died. They show Jessicah trying to catch Marcus's eye. All she wanted were two minutes alone to talk about their relationship. She needed to know whether it was over. But Marcus walked right by without a word. A man used to actions without consequences. Suddenly, she couldn't hold the tears back.

The Moment She Broke
JESS.1 - 8:17PM - You managed to catch his eye. All you wanted were 2 minutes alone with MARCUS to talk about your relationship. You need to know whether it's over for good. But MARCUS just walked right by, without a word. He's a man who's used to actions without consequences. You're no longer worthy of his attention. Suddenly, you can't hold the tears back. A stranger leans over and offers to buy you a cocktail. HA! That's the last thing you need right now.
Jessicah Kane

That's the woman they accused. Not a schemer. Not a calculating killer. Just someone who wanted closure and got a door slammed in her face. By 9:42 PM, she was gone. No one placed her at the scene after that. She ran before anyone could ask what she did next.

But while Jessicah's story ended in silence, Sarah Blackwood's was unraveling.

The Empty Account
MEL.2 - 10:10PM - It was incredibly naive of SARAH to let MARCUS take care of all of the financial dealings in their relationship. You've seen it before. It means that he had every opportunity to prepare for this divorce by building a secret nest egg somewhere else. But SARAH was caught unaware. Face tear-stained, she describes a nearly empty shared bank account and no job or independent income to speak of.
Mel

Sarah, face tear-stained, discovering what Marcus had done to her financially. Then Riley's voicemail surfaced. Drunk, rambling, but devastatingly precise: Marcus had worked with Morgan Reed to set up hidden investment accounts. Cover organizations. Liability shields. Blood money from environmental degradation and insider trading.

The Money Trail Exposed
RILEY.3 - 11:43PM - You call SARAH. You're not sure where she disappeared off to. No answer, so you leave a voice-mail: "MARCUS worked with MORGAN to set up a slew of hidden investment accounts. We're talking cover organizations, liability shields, blood money, environmental degradation, insider trading... I've mapped the shell vehicles. The paper trail is clear. When you're ready, we blow this wide open. The money is yours. Love you! Also... I'm going to find Flip. Don't judge me!"
Riley

Sarah went from victim to potential beneficiary in the span of one voicemail. The money Marcus stole? Hidden in shell accounts, waiting to be recovered. Now I'm getting tips that Sarah is traveling to the Caribbean. To recover from tragic events, they say. To the Caribbean. Where offshore accounts live and die.

The Investigation

Participants have been feeding me updates since waking up after last night's party, and Ashe Motoko's contributions have been so expansive that I consider this article a collaboration. They're my on-the-ground reporter making this story possible in real time.

Sarah dedicated herself to cracking locked boxes containing evidence, working through devastation to find answers. That kind of determination doesn't belong to someone with something to hide.

Alex Reeves and Sam Thorne worked together throughout, united by shared grievance against Marcus. Sam's diary documented Marcus's escalating drug use, a secret chemistry formula, and a warehouse paternity test revealing his infidelity. Alex had the cease and desist letter proving Marcus stole his algorithm.

But an extracted memory reveals Victoria Kingsley had already made her move.

The Corporate Coup
VIC.2 - 9:55PM - You steel yourself before you head to the bar for your meeting. This is not going to be a fun party. But you are going to rip off the band-aid, spill the beans, AND kill two birds with one stone. ALEX is in, MARCUS is out. No more C & D. No more potential criminal conspiracy charges. You've just got to do this, quiet like and ASAP, so that NeurAI remains a viable investment.
Victoria Kingsley

Victoria had decided before the party started. Alex in. Marcus out. Hours later, Marcus was dead. The woman with pockets full of memories moved through that warehouse like someone who owned the narrative no matter how the night shook out.

An anonymous tip was presented during the final moments of the investigation. Sam's laptop showed the code comparison: Zia's Oracle Ledger from 2023 on the left, NeurAI's proprietary system from 2027 on the right. Identical data. Marcus hadn't bothered changing the notes. He wasn't just a thief. He was a sloppy thief.

The Pattern of Violence
SARAH.3 - 11:35PM - ALEX: I'm so sorry - you shouldn't have to deal with us acting like this / SARAH: I somehow I recall breaking up a fight between you two the last time you were at one of his parties too / ALEX: You know why though. He doesn't know how to back down, so he ramps up instead. / SARAH: I do. We've both seen it / ALEX: Then why the hell are you still with him? He's such an asshole! / SARAH: I know he is... but he's my asshole. Now go home. It really was nice to see you, until you tried to break my husband's nose. Again.
Sarah Blackwood

Marcus didn't know how to back down. He ramped up instead. That pattern shows up everywhere: the man who extracted memories without consent, stole algorithms and called it innovation, left his wife with an empty bank account and his pregnant ex-lover without a word.

And through all of it, Jamie "Volt" Woods was watching.

The Bartender's Observations
JAMIE.1 - 8:07PM- These motherfuckers think they run the world. Look at MARCUS and that construction worker he's been hanging around lately. MARCUS just handed over a big fat envelope. You know that's loaded full of Franklins. And MARCUS told you to skip the top shelf drinks tonight. Penny-wise when it comes to his friends, and ultra generous when it comes to his "friends." Whatever. You're definitely noting this interaction down.
Jamie "Volt" Woods

Jamie documented everything. The cash handoffs to Flip. The penny-wise orders to skip top shelf drinks while handing out fat envelopes of Franklins. During the investigation, they collaborated with Ashe Motoko to trace Marcus's multiple identities. Then came the memory Jamie couldn't unsee.

The Deadline
JAMIE.4 - 12:32AM - You're busy restocking when a stranger walks in. They have the wrong vibe for a MARCUS party. "You missed our deadline." MARCUS goes pale. "Tonight we were supposed to do a test run. But you say you're AI's still buggy. So let's try programming my prototype with your memory recordings instead. What's the worst that could happen?" MARCUS: "SKYLER, it's untested technology. What if something goes wrong..." No response. You freeze. Don't breathe. Don't move. BE Invisible. It works. They walk past you like you're furniture.
Jamie "Volt" Woods

Skyler Iyer walked in with the wrong vibe entirely. You missed our deadline. Marcus went pale. They were supposed to do a test run. But Marcus's AI was still buggy. So Skyler suggested programming their prototype with memory recordings instead. What's the worst that could happen? Marcus tried to push back. Untested technology. What if something goes wrong. Skyler didn't respond. Just walked past Jamie like they were furniture.

Jamie told Blake he wanted to sink this bastard. He exposed key memories. But as best as I can tell, beyond Ms Motoko, there were precious few in that room who did not bury inconvenient truths. Others' most intimate memories. Was Jamie one of them? Or was he playing both sides?

Follow the Money

The paternity test proves Marcus is the father. If Jessicah's child inherits, someone profits from Marcus's death. But there's no money trail connecting Jessicah to any shell account. Her alleged inheritance motive exists in the future, not the ledger. The $2.9 million in buried secrets flows to names much closer to home.

Cayman is the largest: $1.455 million across seven transactions from six sources. That seems premeditated and strategic, not simple coincidence.

Rw inc received $780,000. Remi Whitman's initials on the ledger like a calling card. Yet she worked collaboratively with everyone throughout the investigation. Do the initials lie? Was someone setting up Remi? Or was this the confidence of someone who's seen over and over the system is corrupt, and who was finally ready to have a proper payday of their own?

Then there's the account named simply Sarah. $350,000. Someone sold Skyler's memory to an account bearing the widow's name. Was this a setup or was Sarah simply trying to get back what Marcus had already stolen from her?

The Blackmail Attempt
MORGAN.3 - 11:22PM - You're both on the hammock. You show him proof: bribes, fraudulent reports, shell company records, stolen IP... You say: "Go public about the insider trading and take full responsibility or I give all of this damning evidence to Senator Walsh." His face says he's considering it - but there's something else there. Instead of answering, he reaches into his bag and hands you a generous bribe. MARCUS: "Take care of it, will you? Just like you always do," then leaves without giving you a chance to respond.
Morgan Reed

Everyone had been getting their due through Marcus' shady dealings, so, why shouldn't Sarah get to walk away from tragedy with a little compensation? Especially if she knew about Marcus' baby, and what that meant for her share of his estate now.

The Players

Ashe Motoko fed me evidence all night. Their collaboration with Jamie traced Marcus's multiple identities. Their work has been so significant that I consider this article a collaboration.

Remi Whitman moved between tasks, working with everyone. They discovered hidden cash in pillows with Alex. But Rw inc bears their initials, and $780,000 is a lot of money to explain away.

Alex and Sam worked together throughout, united by shared grievance. When Remi joined them to find that hidden cash, three people wronged were working in concert. A stark contrast to the conspiracies that led to the chaos of last night, instead, finally some solidarity.

Victoria Kingsley played cards close. Former internship supervisor to Marcus, Alex, and Sarah. Now VC for both NeurAI and AIBioComp. She moved through that warehouse like someone who owned the narrative no matter how things shook out.

Sarah's grief surfaced as buried memories resurfaced. But her dedication to cracking those locked boxes eliminated her as a suspect. The widow who lost everything to Marcus's manipulation now has Riley's map to the shell vehicles.

And Jessicah Kane? Accused despite her absence. Her exposed memories show emotional devastation, not calculation. She wanted closure. She got accused of murder. Perhaps I am wrong, and she really is to be blamed for Marcus' untimely demise, but this story seems far too cleanly concluded for everyone involved. We will have to wait and find out what further investigation reveals.

What's Missing

Where is Jessicah Kane now? No one placed her at the party after 9:42 PM. She ran before anyone could ask.

What did Sarah remember when those buried memories resurfaced? All her own memories stayed buried. Whatever she knows about her husband's final hours, she's not sharing.

What was Sam's secret chemistry formula? The diary mentions it alongside Marcus's drug use. Quinn confessed something to Ezra about a compound. Psychotrophin-3A. Marcus was experimenting on himself. Jessicah knew for a fact.

Jamie seemed to want justice, but needed cash. Especially with his gig from Marcus now at an end. It's hard to imagine, with all that he saw while going unseen, and kept his employers' secrets, that he was one of the few who chose to expose truths beyond the justice he sought out when he said he waned to "sink that bastard."

Who coordinates Cayman? Seven transactions, six sources. $1.455 million buys a lot of quiet.

The gaps form a pattern. Everyone close to Marcus buried something. The people who exposed the most also buried the most. And the woman accused of murder left almost no trace at all. The one source I feel I can truly trust was Ashe who'd spent the night interviewing attendees at the party.

Marcus Blackwood extracted memories from people without their consent. Then he extracted their financial security. Their work. Their dignity. When he died, someone started trading those stolen memories. Buying silence. Selling secrets. The technology that violated privacy became a currency.

The first surveillance was Marcus watching his partygoers. The second was the partygoers watching each other. The third was whoever controls those shell accounts, watching everyone, with Blake moving all the pieces around the chessboard on behalf of his overlords. Funders behind NeurAI's technology beyond Vic's investments.

The group accused Jessicah Kane last night. Pregnant, abandoned, absent. The inheritance motive is clean on paper. But the money trails don't lead to her. They lead to sophisticated shell networks, competing founders, and a bartender who saw everything and sold most of it. And what a convenient coincidence that the accused was not one of those who were present, with access to the ability to bury all inconvenient truths.

The shape of silence has a price tag. This one cost the bosses at NeurAI $2.9 million.

The tips are still coming in. And somewhere out there, a woman is carrying Marcus Blackwood's child, accused of his murder, while the people who actually knew what happened are traveling to the Caribbean, cashing checks, and hoping the silence holds.
