Two Fled to the Pacific. Fifteen Woke Up With Holes Where Their Memories Should Be.

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Victoria Kingsley and Morgan Reed are running. And the memory extraction business is just getting started.

by Cassandra Nova · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-122125

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Victoria Kingsley and Morgan Reed are somewhere over the Pacific, unreachable by the authorities now pursuing them. Fifteen people woke up this morning with holes where their memories should be. Someone extracted those fragments without permission. Someone locked them away. And someone made sure Marcus wouldn't be around to explain why.

I was in that room when the accusations flew. Two camps. Two theories. One side, led by Diana Nilsson, pushed for Derek Thorne, the party architect with the drugs in his gym bag. The other side pushed for Victoria and Morgan. An intense debate. Then a vote. Then two people running for the airport.

The group made their choice. Victoria and Morgan made theirs. Now I'm going to tell you what I saw, what got exposed, and what someone paid over four million dollars to keep buried.

The Story

It started before the party even began. Jamie Woods was stocking the top shelf when the back door opened. Wrong vibe. Guy in a suit, the kind of calm that comes from knowing exactly how much damage you can do.

The Threat That Started Everything
JAV003 - 4:18PM - Stocking the top shelf when the back door opens. Guy has the wrong vibe for a MARCUS party. "We need privacy." MARCUS goes pale. Suit's voice carries: "Tonight's the test run. At least forty extracts. Exactly as instructed or YOU become my problem to solve." Silence. Then MARCUS, shaky: "What if something goes wrong—" No response. You freeze. Don't breathe. Don't move. BE Invisible. Door opens. Guy walks past you like you're furniture.
Jamie Woods

Forty extracts. That's what Blake demanded. Forty people's private memories, ripped out without consent. Marcus went pale. Jamie went invisible. I have spent months wondering what happens at those parties. Now I know.

But here's what makes this story complicated. While Blake was threatening Marcus in the back room, Taylor Chase was at the bar, drunk enough to brag. Howie Sullivan was the unlucky audience.

The Drunken Confession
TAC001 - 1:22am - TAYLOR drunkenly brags to HOWIE about getting ASHE fired from the Silicon Valley Business Journal
Taylor Chase

Ashe Motoko. The journalist who'd been investigating Marcus for years. The one who got legally gagged, career destroyed, expose buried. And Taylor was bragging about making it happen. I watched Taylor's face during the investigation. No shame. Just the confidence of someone who's gotten away with it before.

Meanwhile, behind the bar, Diana Nilsson heard something else entirely. Morgan Reed cornering Marcus. The lobbyist and her client, having a conversation neither expected anyone to overhear.

The Blackmail
DIN021 - 1:58AM - Behind the bar. MORGAN to MARCUS: 'Senator Walsh has receipts. YOUR receipts. Cooperate or I tell her everything.' MARCUS: 'Try it.' MORGAN: 'I'm your only friend in Washington. Remember that.' Neither sees you. Both should.
Diana Nilsson

Senator Walsh has receipts. Morgan had leverage. And Marcus, cocky as ever, told her to try it. Three hours later, Marcus was dead. Coincidence? I've been doing this job too long to believe in those.

Kai Andersen was somewhere else entirely that night. Searching. Panicking. They'd brought Ashe to the party to impress them with this world. By 1 AM, both were slipping away.

The Search
KAA002 - 01:00AM - The bass drowns thought. Garden, rooftop, studio—no ASHE. Someone saw them with VICTORIA. Another says MARCUS. Across the room, FLIP's with RACHEL. Face a hollow mask of confidence, and body moving spasmodically like an untethered marionette. You brought ASHE to impress them with your world. Now both are slipping away.
Kai Andersen

Someone saw Ashe with Victoria. Someone else said Marcus. Kai searched the garden, the rooftop, the studio. Nothing. And across the room, Flip was falling apart, Rachel Torres trying to hold him together. Everyone fragmenting. Everyone losing something.

Which brings us to Derek Thorne. The party architect. The man with psychotrophin3b in his gym bag. The memory extraction drug. Marcus wanted Derek on the NeurAI payroll. A new opportunity, Marcus called it. A way to commercialize the product.

The Business Plan
MAB002 - 07/15/2026 - A new opportunity. Everyone loves DEREK's new drug. And NOW, someone out there wants to commercialize the product. Go legit. ALEX's tech will take NeurAI only so far. THIS offer might be the way to a unicorn. Now… you just have to get DEREK to heel.
Marcus Blackwood

Derek's drug. Alex's tech. Marcus's ambition. And someone out there wanting to commercialize it all. I watched the investigation nearly land on Derek. He designed the parties where this happened. He had the means. He was the obvious choice. But obvious isn't the same as guilty. Sometimes obvious is just convenient.

James Whitman saw something too. Right before losing consciousness, right before his own memory got extracted, he watched Marcus entering a secret room adjoining the party space.

The Last Sighting
JAW011 - 4:36AM - Right before losing consciousness and memory, JAMES sees MARCUS entering a secret room adjoining the party space.
James Whitman

4:36 AM. The last confirmed sighting of Marcus alive. James saw it. James, who never spoke to Blake all evening despite Blake's efforts. James, who was furious that Victoria gave Marcus twenty million while ignoring his own company. James kept that memory. He also kept his distance from the money.

And then there's the moment that ties it all together. Jamie again, out for a smoke, catching Victoria in a panic with the man who'd just punched Marcus.

The Job Offer
JAV042 - 12:17AM - Can't even get a peaceful smoke in. The dude who punched MARCUS is out here with that VC lady, VICTORIA. She's in a panic. They are trying to be discreet, but the wind carries the sentence "He's out. You're in. Trust me. Please just come inside." Intense back and forth. She convinces the punching man to head back inside.
Jamie Woods

He's out. You're in. Victoria was offering Alex Reeves the job before Marcus was even cold. That's not grief. That's not shock. That's a plan already in motion. Blake threatened Marcus at 4:18 PM. Victoria was already replacing him by 12:17 AM. And Morgan was blackmailing him at 1:58 AM. The timeline tells its own story.

Follow the Money

Four point one one million dollars. That's what silence cost at this party. Six shell accounts. Sixteen transactions. And patterns that make you wonder who knew what, and when.

Gorlan received $1.125 million across six transactions. The largest single account. Systematic. Patient. Someone running a burial operation, not making panic moves. Victoria's name appears nowhere on the ledger. But her fingerprints are on the funding that made NeurAI possible. Morgan's lobbying work gave her access to everything worth burying.

Dominic pulled in $960,000. Including last minute activity, Kai running to the Valet during heightened monitoring. I watched it happen. The timing suggests panic, not planning. Someone realized too late what they needed to hide.

ChaseT. $750,000. Taylor's last name is Chase. The account emerged after Offbeat went silent. Offbeat, which activated early with a $50,000 first burial bonus and then nothing. Diana and Taylor started the evening together at Blake's station. Then they split. Whatever partnership they had fractured mid-investigation. ChaseT looks like Taylor going solo. Or someone wanting it to look that way.

John D. stayed anonymous throughout. $625,000 from someone with enough insider knowledge to stay hidden while everyone else was exposed. James never spoke to Blake. Not once. Strategic distance from the money trail. But John D. knew things. John D. moved with purpose.

Fun received $450,000. A single large transaction connected to Derek's positioning. Someone was paying to make Derek look guilty. The fall guy doesn't choose himself.

The money tells a story. Not what was buried. I can't see that. But who was buying silence, and how much they were willing to pay for it.

The Players

Diana Nilsson led the final accusation against Victoria and Morgan. She saw them working together all night. She heard Morgan's blackmail behind the bar. But Diana was also Sarah Blackwood's divorce lawyer. She had her own reasons to want Marcus gone. I'm not saying she did anything wrong. I'm saying everyone in that room had angles.

Jamie Woods saw everything. The bartender who froze when Blake threatened Marcus. Who caught Victoria's job offer on a smoke break. Who brought those memories to my attention instead of Blake's ledger. Jamie could have buried what they knew. They didn't.

"Tonight's the test run. At least forty extracts. Exactly as instructed or YOU become my problem to solve."

— Blake, overheard by Jamie Woods

James Whitman kept strategic distance. Never spoke to Blake despite Blake's rapport building efforts throughout the evening. Saw Marcus entering the secret room. Had a rival's motive. Victoria chose Marcus over AIBioComp, and James was furious about that twenty million. But he kept his hands clean. No transactions. No compromises. Just watching.

Taylor and Kai took Blake's money. I watched Taylor at the Valet station. Confident. Unbothered. The same confidence that let them brag about destroying Ashe's career. And 'Dominic's' last minute transactions...I'm not saying it was Kai. But we all saw him run to Blake in those final moments. Looked to me like someone who suddenly realized what they needed to hide.

But not everyone chose profit. Last night showed me there are still honest folks in this valley. Folks who brought the stolen memories they found forward so that the public would know what happened here last night. Some of those memories were damning. Some of them painful. All of them costly. The system offered them money to stay quiet. They chose truth instead.

"He's out. You're in. Trust me."

— Victoria Kingsley, overheard by Jamie Woods

Two camps emerged in the final debate. One pushed for Derek. The party architect. The drug supplier. The obvious choice. The other pushed for Victoria and Morgan. The investor and the lobbyist. The money and the access. Diana led the first camp. And Diana lost.

What's Missing

Victoria and Morgan fled the country. Authorities are pursuing them. But I don't have a single exposed memory showing them planning Marcus's death. Only the aftermath. Only the cleanup. Only Morgan's blackmail and Victoria's job offer. The shape of guilt, but not the proof.

What happened in the secret room James saw Marcus enter? That's the last sighting. And then nothing. Whatever happened next, whoever was waiting in that room, it got extracted along with everything else. And it got buried.

Why did Taylor and Diana's partnership fracture? Offbeat went silent. ChaseT emerged. Something happened between them that neither is talking about. The money split tells a story. The silence tells a bigger one.

What was Kai buying with those last minute transactions? $10,000 to Dominic at 8:49 PM. Moments before the authorities arrived on the scene, only to find Blake there. Waiting pass along the 'official' story. Panic timing. But panic about what? What did Kai suddenly realize needed hiding?

Gorlan. $1.125 million. Six transactions. The biggest account, the most systematic operation. I can see the totals. I can't see the content. Someone paid a fortune to keep something dark. The shape of the silence tells me something happened. The content stays locked away.

They say all roads lead to Rome. And in the end all of the financial trails lead to one place. NeurAI. Blake's employers. A faceless equity firm, sitting on the board, and calling the shots through Blake's offers of cash for 'discretion'. And perhaps this was the beginning of the fall of Rome. And hopefully not the marker of its rise into full imperial glory.

Victoria Kingsley and Morgan Reed are somewhere over the Pacific. Authorities are pursuing. The accusation stands: collusion to murder Marcus Blackwood. But an accusation isn't a conviction. And distance isn't the same as innocence.

Derek Thorne nearly took the fall. Two camps argued for hours. In the end, the group chose Victoria and Morgan. But Derek designed those parties. He had the drugs. The question isn't whether he's innocent. It's whether he was convenient.

First they wanted your clicks. Then your data. Now they want what it felt like to be you. Memory extraction isn't a murder mystery. It's a business model. Marcus Blackwood didn't invent it. He just took it to its logical conclusion. And fifteen people woke up this morning to discover they'd been the product all along.

I was in that room. I watched the arguments, the alliances, the fractures. I saw people choose truth over money, and money over truth. I watched a group of strangers decide who should pay for what was done to them. They made their choice. Victoria and Morgan made theirs.

Somewhere over the Pacific, two people are running. Here in San Francisco, fifteen people are trying to remember what was taken from them. And I'm telling you: this is just the beginning.
