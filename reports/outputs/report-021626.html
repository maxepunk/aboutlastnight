The Man Who Wasn't There

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Fifteen people woke up with stolen memories. And the accused killer vanished before anyone could ask why.

Casey Nova | Senior Investigative Reporter · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-0216-26

Marcus Blackwood is dead. Fifteen people woke up with holes where their memories should be. Someone extracted those memories without permission. Someone locked them away. And someone made sure Marcus wouldn't be explaining why.

The group accused Oliver Sterling. A man who wasn't there when they woke up. A man whose research made the extraction possible. A man Marcus had by the throat for years.

But here's what keeps me up at night: every memory we recovered came from a party designed to steal them. So how do we trust anything we found?

The Story

I want to start with the people who actually wanted the truth. Rachel Torres and Ashe Motoko spent the investigation like they were running out of time. Because they were. We all were.

Rachel had been mapping the money for months. In a voicemail to Sarah Blackwood, captured in one of her extracted memories, she laid it out: shell vehicles, liability shields, blood money flowing through Morgan Reed's energy clients. She'd found the trail. She just needed someone to walk it with her.

The Voicemail That Started It All
RAT031 - 12:15AM - Calling SARAH. Not sure where she disappeared off to. No answer. Leave a voice-mail: 'He was with counsel for one of Morgan Reed's energy clients, talking "liability shields." Blood money, Sarah. I've mapped the shell vehicles. When you're ready, we do this the right way. Love you. Also... going to find Flip. Don't judge me.'
Rachel Torres

Ashe brought her own scars to this investigation. Years ago, she'd written an expose on Marcus's memory experiments. BizAI, the company that became NeurAI. Taylor Chase got her fired for it. The story was legally erased. Her career was collateral damage.

The extracted memory shows the moment her lawyer broke the news: fight and go bankrupt, or settle and stay silent. She settled. But she never stopped knowing what she knew.

The Story They Killed
ASM042 - 03/20/2020 - Lawyer's advice: 'Fight and go bankrupt, or settle and stay silent.' NDA forbids you from writing about MARCUS or BizAI. Ever. Your expose about his crimes? Legally erased. Your career? Collateral damage. His victory.
Ashe Motoko

Here's the thing about Ashe: she had dated Alex Reeves in the past. The man Marcus stole everything from. The man Victoria Kingsley was positioning to take Marcus's job. Then, I watched them work together during the investigation, Ashe and Alex, and I couldn't tell where truth-seeking ended and personal stake began. Maybe there's no difference.

But the truth-seekers were chasing something bigger than one dead CEO. They were chasing an empire.

Marcus Blackwood didn't just run a company. He ran a leverage operation. At least nine people at that party had reason to want him dead. Jessicah Kane was pregnant with his child. He blew her off every time she tried to talk about it.

Dismissed
JEK031 - 9:20PM - You NEED to talk to MARCUS about the baby, He blows you off yet again. All you wanted were two minutes. HOWIE has overheard. He offers a few kind words, and a glass of water. The last thing you need right now.
Jessicah Kane

Flip owed Marcus money. Sofia Francisco knew the AI was fraudulent. Eighty-seven percent of Marcus's 'breakthrough' required manual fixes. She showed Leila Bishara the specs and offered a choice: expose it or leverage it first. The truth, apparently, is worth what someone will pay.

And then there was the $14.5 million. Three Blackwood shell companies funneling money to Morgan Reed's projects, timed perfectly before EPA exemptions, zoning variances, permits. Rachel found the trail. The trail led straight to the dead man's door.

The Fraud Behind the Demo
SOF003 - 01/31/2027 - The demo loops perfectly. The code doesn't. MARCUS's 'breakthrough AI' needs manual fixes for 87% of inputs. You show LEILA the specs. 'Your film could expose this... or leverage it first.' Party venues were his test sites. Truth is worth what someone will pay.
Sofia Francisco

Sofia was certain, at first, that Victoria Kingsley was behind it all. I watched her make the case during the investigation. Victoria had the motive. Victoria had the access. Victoria was already making moves before Marcus was cold.

The memory comes from Jamie Woods, who couldn't even get a peaceful smoke break. Outside, Victoria was in a panic with the man who'd punched Marcus earlier that night. Alex Reeves. The wind carried her voice: 'He's out. You're in. Trust me. Please just come inside.'

The Offer Outside
JAV042 - 12:17AM - Can't even get a peaceful smoke in. The dude who punched MARCUS is out here with that VC lady, VICTORIA. She's in a panic. They are trying to be discreet, but the wind carries the sentence "He's out. You're in. Trust me. Please just come inside." Intense back and forth. She convinces the punching man to head back inside.
Jamie "Volt" Woods

Victoria had poured $163 million into NeurAI. James Whitman was furious she'd funded Marcus over his company, AIBioComp. Alex had watched Marcus steal his life's work. They'd all been circling each other at that party, and by 12:17 AM, they were already positioning for the aftermath.

But Sofia's certainty cracked. The Oliver story emerged, and it was too good to ignore.

This is where it gets complicated. The party wasn't just a party. It was a test site. Marcus had commissioned something called a Neuro-Aesthetic Protocol. Tori Zhang pitched it as 'immersive neural stimulation for party environments.' Marcus put on the headset, grinned like a kid, and paid fifty thousand dollars to deploy it.

The Breakthrough That Wasn't
TOZ002 - 09/30/24 - MARCUS wants "immersive neural stimulation for party environments." You pitch Synesthesia Engine. He lights up. Puts on the headset. Thirty seconds: grinning like a kid. "How soon?" "Six weeks" "Fifty thousand for Neuro-Aesthetic Protocol. Full deployment." VALIDATION. The tech WORKS. With his endorsement, VICTORIA will reconsider. This is the breakthrough. You don't know yet: this is the peak. Everything after is downhill.
Tori Zhang

That was 2024. By 2027, the tech had evolved. Psychotrophin-3B. Memory extraction at scale. And Marcus was following instructions from someone. The memory shows him at 3:52 AM, trying to get drugged partygoers to lock away their own stolen memories. Derek Thorne wandered off mid-command. Sarah Blackwood stared at a wall, tears streaming.

The Instructions
MAB003 - 3:52AM - The instructions were clear: "Make them secure the tokens. Combinations only you know. Call when done." Simple. The extractions worked. Now you just need these drugged zombies to LOCK them. "DEREK take this token. Lock it the" He wanders off mid-command. SARAH's staring at a wall, tears streaming. This is chaos. Don't think ANYONE has the right combinations anymore. Drugged people make terrible assistants. And THEY WILL be back if you don't call soon.
Marcus Blackwood

'THEY want you to remove the safety,' another memory reveals. 'When the memories expire, they are returned to their owners. THEY would rather the memory is destroyed.' Marcus refused that part. Small mercy. But someone was giving him orders. Someone was on the other end of that call.

And that brings us to Oliver Sterling.

Oliver wasn't there when they woke up. But his fingerprints are all over this. Years ago, he ran a company with James Whitman and Leila Bishara. Two years of building something real. Then Oliver burned it down to bury his research.

The Betrayal
OLS002 - 03/15/18 - Conference call. You, JAMES, and LEILA. Two years building something real. You burned it down to bury your research. LEILA: "I trusted you." JAMES walked with you, said you were right. You wiped everything. Lost her. Lost what the three of you built. Told yourself it was worth it. Then MARCUS came knocking.
Oliver Sterling

Leila never forgave the betrayal. 'I trusted you.' Those three words hung in the air when her memory played. James walked with Oliver, said he was right. But Oliver wiped everything. Lost what they built. Told himself it was worth it.

Then Marcus came knocking. Human neural organoids research. Psychotrophin-3B. Marcus blackmailed Oliver to continue the experiments, to develop an ingestion method. 'Now he's got you by the balls,' the memory reads. 'When people get hurt, there will be blood on your hands.'

The Blackmail
OLS011 - 08/22/26 - Your human neural organoids research. MARCUS blackmailed you to continue with the experiments to use Psychotrophin-3B and your organoid research to develop an ingestion method. Why did you bury the PT-3B safety assessments? Now he's got you by the balls and when people get hurt. There will be blood on your hands.
Oliver Sterling

Oliver had motive. Marcus had him trapped. Oliver had means. His research made the extraction possible. Oliver had opportunity. He disappeared while everyone else was drugged.

The group accused him. It's the story that goes on the record. The police are not looking for Oliver. But here's what I can't shake: the evidence that condemns Oliver was extracted by a system he helped build. Under blackmail. For a man who wanted to destroy memories, not return them. Every memory we found came from a party designed to steal them.

Follow the Money

The Black Market ledger tells its own story. Nine shell accounts. Over $2.9 million paid for silence. I can tell you the shape of the silence. I can't tell you what's inside it.

Harris took $900,000 across two transactions. The first came at 8:01 PM, the second at 8:33 PM. Someone paid nearly a million dollars to keep something quiet. The name tells me nothing.

Pumpkin is the single largest burial. $750,000 in one transaction at 7:57 PM. Whatever was in that memory, it was worth three-quarters of a million to make disappear. That's not spare change. That's desperation.

CEO. Two transactions totaling $475,000. Someone with a sense of irony, or someone pointing a finger. The account name feels like a message. I just don't know who it's for. And here's the thing. As the investigation wound down, 1 disappeared off the board. And 'CEO' had their numbers swell to over $1.3 Million. Collusion? Conspiracy? It seems so, but the evidence is sparse, and I cannot be confident.

Lefty. Two transactions, $250,000. Cryptic name, clear money trail. The amounts aren't huge by this ledger's standards, but they add up to a mid-level tech salary. For two memories.

Marcus. Two transactions under the dead man's name. $200,000 total. Someone's either framing or mocking. Either way, it's a choice.

James. $150,000 in one transaction at 7:55 PM. James Whitman was furious Victoria funded Marcus over AIBioComp. Interesting choice of account name.

Rachel mapped the shell vehicles months before this night. She found where the money came from. Now we know where some of it went. The trail leads both directions, and neither end looks clean.

The Players

Let me tell you about the ones who brought evidence to light.

Rachel Torres called out Blake multiple times. Heated exchanges about NeurAI's practices, about what the Valet's employer was really doing. She didn't back down. She'd been tracking this story longer than any of us, and she wasn't going to let corporate discretion win.

Ashe Motoko brought her expertise despite the NDA that silenced her years ago. Her expose was legally erased, her memories stolen, but her integrity was intact. She contributed what she could, and she did it knowing Taylor Chase, the person who got her fired, was in the same room.

Sofia Francisco started certain about Victoria's guilt. I watched her make the case, watched her conviction waver as the Oliver story emerged. She exposed evidence that showed the AI fraud, that Marcus's breakthrough was mostly manual labor. That takes something. Admitting your own employer's product is a lie.

"He's out. You're in. Trust me. Please just come inside."

— Victoria Kingsley, from Jamie's extracted memory

Jamie Woods exposed memories about the party's true purpose. The smoke break memory that caught Victoria and Alex outside. The observation about Marcus handing fat envelopes to DC types. Jamie saw more than most, and shared more than they had to.

Alex Reeves, Flip, Howie Sullivan. They all contributed. Leila's recovered memory proved Marcus stole her Oracle Ledger code. Alex's cease and desist letter came across my desk, demanding NeurAI stop using his stolen algorithm. The evidence built a picture none of us could have assembled alone.

Then there were those who took Blake's money. The accounts are anonymous for the most part. But I have my guesses. Victoria Kingsley. Derek Thorne. Diana Nilsson. Taylor Chase. All had their reasons to bury evidence. I don't know if they did. But I do know quite a few people got paid.

I'm not going to pretend I don't understand why some memories stayed off my board. You wake up with gaps in your head, someone offers you cash to keep those gaps private? That's not greed. That's survival. The system made the choice hard. Don't blame people for making it.

"I trusted you."

— Leila Bishara, from Oliver's extracted memory

Sarah Blackwood appeared genuinely upset by Marcus's death. She rebuffed Blake's charming attempts multiple times. Whatever she felt about her husband, it wasn't simple. The memory of her resignation letter, rewritten five times, tells a story about sacrifice that doesn't have a clean ending.

Blake pulled Alex aside for a private conversation. Alex and Victoria displayed friendly behavior throughout. The alliances formed fast. The investigation hadn't even started, and people were already picking sides.

What's Missing

Oliver Sterling disappeared before anyone woke up. Where is he now? If he killed Marcus, why didn't he destroy the evidence that implicates him? The accused murderer left behind the very memories that condemn him. That's either arrogance or a setup.

Marcus had leverage on at least nine people at that party. Any of them could have wanted him dead. Why did the group settle on Oliver? Maybe because he's the only one who isn't here to defend himself.

Alex and Victoria coordinated before the party. She was in a panic outside, telling the man who punched Marcus that he was in, Marcus was out. How deep did their planning go? Victoria's $163 million was on the line. Alex's life's work had been stolen. They had every reason to want Marcus gone.

The party was designed to extract memories. Marcus was following instructions from someone. 'THEY want you to remove the safety.' Who are THEY? The memory doesn't say. Marcus was taking orders, and whoever was giving them is still out there.

Ashe's unpublished expose on memory experiments was legally erased. Taylor got her fired from Silicon Valley Business Journal. What did Ashe know that was worth destroying her career? The NDA forbids her from writing about Marcus or BizAI. Ever. But the truth she found didn't disappear just because the story did.

Marcus's body is missing. The murder scene has no corpse. A 'mystery person' approached Marcus multiple times. There were demands to meet with a world leader. The mytery person was never identified. NeurAI is rumored to be getting ready to announce a new CEO...days after Marcus' death. The timing is suspicious. Start-ups move fast. But this is something else entirely.

Someone was giving Marcus instructions. 'Make them secure the tokens. Combinations only you know. Call when done.' Who was on the other end of that call? The extraction worked. The chaos was by design. And somewhere, someone is waiting for a phone to ring.

The group accused Oliver Sterling of murdering Marcus Blackwood. They believe he escaped while the rest were drugged. That's their conclusion, earned through investigation. They were in that room. They pieced together the fragments. They made a call. And now the police have their prime suspect. They are on the hunt. Oliver, officially a fugitive.

But here's what I keep thinking about: fifteen people woke up with their memories stolen. Not metaphorically. Actually extracted. And the first thing that happened? Someone started buying those memories. Paying for silence. Turning human experience into a transaction.

That's not a murder mystery. That's a business model.

First they wanted your clicks. Then your conversations. Now they want what it felt like to be you. Marcus Blackwood didn't invent surveillance capitalism. He just took it to its logical conclusion: extracting human experience and selling it to the highest bidder. The party wasn't a crime scene. It was a proof of concept.

Marcus is dead. Maybe Oliver did it. Maybe someone else. The technology still exists. NeurAI will soon have a new CEO. Whoever was giving Marcus instructions is still out there. The empire didn't die with him.

Fifteen people. Nine blackmail victims. One body. And the accused wasn't even in the room.

I was in that room. I watched them piece together the fragments. And I'm telling you: this is just the beginning.
