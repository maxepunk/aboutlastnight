## A — Accuracy errors

### A1. Mel's divorce-client timeline off by ~24 hours (HIGH)

**Article (THE STORY):** "By 12:50 AM, Mel had called an emergency Stanford Four meeting and was openly weighing blackmail to recover Marcus's money for Sarah. By 2:08 AM, Mel had taken Sarah on as a divorce client."

**Source (Mel's emails about divorce):** Email is dated `Sent: Feb 21, 2027, 2:08 AM`. The party is Feb 21 evening. The investigation morning is Feb 22.

Feb 21 2:08 AM was the early hours of the **party day**, ~18 hours BEFORE the party started. The article places "by 2:08 AM" in the same night chronology as the 12:50 AM Stanford Four meeting (mel004), implying Mel took Sarah on AFTER calling the Stanford Four meeting. Wrong by a full day. Mel was already Sarah's divorce attorney coming INTO the party.

This is the same temporal trap that 051626 A1 hit. Pipeline temporal-conflation pattern persists.

**Fix:** "By 12:50 AM, Mel had called an emergency Stanford Four meeting and was openly weighing blackmail to recover Marcus's money for Sarah. The legal machinery had been moving for almost a day already. Mel's email to her partners at Patchwork Law Firm, announcing she had taken Sarah on as a client effective immediately, was timestamped 2:08 AM the morning of the party."

### A2. "Unanimous" vote claim contradicts the article's own tally (HIGH)

**Location 1 — Photo #14 caption:** "...the woman who moved the room from a Vic-Zia tie to a unanimous Zia."

**Location 2 — Closing (C5):** "I am saying Vic Kingsley pivoted a tie into a unanimous accusation and then left the country."

The vote was 12-3-1 with 2 abstentions. **Not unanimous.** The lede and C1 ("Twelve people voted for Zia Bishara") are correct. Two later instances ("unanimous") contradict.
