Quinn Sterling Convicted in Absentia for Marcus Blackwood's Death.

Twelve of Marcus Blackwood's guests gathered to investigate his death this morning. Their verdict landed on a suspect who was seen at last night's party, but gone by the time everyone else woke up. An active manhunt is now underway.

Cassandra Nova | NovaNews | Senior Investigative Correspondent || Ashe Motoko | Guest Reporter · February 22, 2027 · Article NNA-052326-26

Quinn Sterling wasn't there. The chemist the room finally settled on for Marcus Blackwood's death had somehow left the party after the others had lost consciousness. Twelve people stood around the evidence table this morning, sifted through extracted memories and labeled baggies of Psychotrophin, and named him despite not knowing where he is or how he disappeared from the warehouse where they all woke up this morning.

I wasn't in that room. Ashe Motoko was, and he shares this byline because he did the feet-on-the-ground reporting while I worked the wires from outside. By the time the vote landed, Quinn Sterling was already a fugitive in his own absence.

The Story

Ashe took the stage at the start of the final deliberations and broke it down systematically. The drugs killed Marcus. Someone named Quinn made the drugs. Quinn was gone by morning. The chemistry argument tracked, and the rest of the room let it carry.

The Blackmail Hook
QUINN.2 - 9:59PM - Somehow, MARCUS found out about the unusual side effects of Psychotrophin3A, and is obsessed with its ability to induce neural plasticity in large doses. Why did you bury the PT-3A safety assessments? Now he's got you by the balls and is blackmailing you to continue with your experiments to develop it. This drug is dangerous, and should not be in the wrong hands. But MARCUS wants you to bring him another batch of it tonight. You're to meet him at the bar at 10.
Quinn Sterling

A recovered memory placed Quinn at the bar on Marcus's instruction the night before, terrified of what he was being asked to deliver. The blackmail trail surfaced next. Marcus had him by the throat over buried safety assessments for Psychotrophin-3A, and was already drafting the demand for another batch.

The Demand
To: quinn.k.sterling@gmail.com From: marcus.blackwood@neurai.co Date: February 17, 2027 Subject: Re: the latest trial I need you to bring me another batch of Psychotrophin-3A. The ingestion method you established with the last trial is effective, but the side effects I am looking for are totally inconsistent. I know you're resistant because you didn't test levels anywhere near this high during the PT-3B safety assessments, but I'm certain we should go up on the dosage. And you can't afford to say no, can you? I'm having a party on the 21st. I'll put you on the guest list. Come find me there. Anything you want to tell me about it, you can tell me in person.
Marcus Blackwood

In the final minutes, the room voted. 'This asshole got me fired,' Ashe told the group. 'Alex was seen punching him, but none of that was reason for us to have killed him. If there's someone to blame, I don't know. But Quinn made this drug, and Quinn is not here. We know that.' Jamie 'Volt' Woods added that he'd found an empty capsule at the bar that fit Ashe's drug theory. The room let the argument carry.

Before Quinn's name landed, two others did. Marcus's theft of Alex Reeves's NeurAI code came up first, and Alex's name went on the board. Then Alex made an impassioned defense, and then revealed that Blake had been working the room, offering up Marcus' old job to a select few if they could prove they had NeurAI's best interests at heart. She admitted she'd been approached, and then named Sarah Blackwood as another candidate who had been approached. Sarah's name went up next. A showdown between the two women split the room into two camps.

Alex had brought paper. The Patchwork Law Firm's cease and desist letter was already in motion when Marcus died, demanding NeurAI's board oust him and install Alex as CTO. Mel Nilsson, Sarah's divorce lawyer and another old Stanford friend of Marcus's, was running a parallel front through the family court.

The Cease and Desist
Patchwork LAW FIRM patchworklawfirm@gmail.com January 28, 2027 Re: NeurAI Cease and Desist To the Board of Directors of NeurAI: The Patchwork Law firm has been retained by Alex Reeves in a suit against Marcus Blackwood and NeurAI regarding the theft of intellectual property in the formation and code base of NeurAI... ILLUMINATION of our assertions can be found in the NeurAI code base documentation and coding architecture, which aligns with A. Reeves's programming style, and not with M. Blackwood's nonexistent programming technique. We assert that the core concepts of NeurAI, as well as the foundational code, are based on the sole intellectual property of A. Reeves... We insist that NeurAI immediately cease and desist from using any of A. Reeves's IP. A. Reeves should be granted all the equity in the company that M. Blackwood currently holds. Furthermore, we insist that M. Blackwood be removed as the Chief Executive Officer of NeurAI.
Alex Reeves

An anonymous leak from NeurAI's board reached me after the deliberations closed. The email commended Alex on her negotiation skills this morning and made her an offer to take on the role of interim CEO alongside Sarah, shared leadership for two women who had had a showdown to prove their innocence during the final moments of this morning's investigation. The leak confirmed what Alex had admitted in her own defense: the investigation was being run as an interview in plain sight.

The chemistry case the room built against Quinn opened back up the moment anyone really looked at the baggies. Psychotrophin-1A through 10E. A whole formulation tree, not a single compound. Sam Thorne's name came up as the source of the recreational supply at Marcus's parties; her own recovered diary, exposed this morning, places her squarely inside Marcus's drug supply. "It's probably the shit I'm making for him," she wrote of Marcus three days before the party. The PT-3B batch that extracted everyone's memories last night was Sam's reformulation of Quinn's original synthesis. She's in the Stanford Four. So is Mel. So was Marcus.

What Marcus Said When He Was Too High to Lie
SAM.2 - 10:44PM - God's favorite idiot. Jesus Christ, MARCUS is so high! And you just gave him your usual party blend. He should not be so out of his mind that told you that! The BizAI algorithm wasn't his own work. ALEX rewrote the whole thing and MARCUS somehow still took credit. Is he feeling remorse? No. This is MARCUS we're talking about. You bite your tongue and take another hit.
Sam Thorne

Sam was in the building. The room flagged her as the recreational supplier but no one voted her as a suspect. Her diary recorded her worry about him days before the party. When the drug thread put her name in play during deliberations, she said it again. Her own recovered memory from 10:44 PM shows Marcus, drugged out of his mind on her usual blend, confessing the BizAI algorithm was Alex's work. Mel Nilsson's recovered memory walks through Sarah's nearly empty shared bank account; her email to her law firm shows the divorce was already a retained case the morning Marcus died. The chemist in the room helped convict the chemist who wasn't.

While the chemistry argument ran, a different network was operating. Ashe relayed that Morgan Reed was a fixture at Blake's station this morning. In fact, Morgan was the first person who was overheard walking up to Blake saying that he had a piece of evidence to expose. Yet, despite all that, somehow he added nothing to the case against Marcus's lobbying architecture.

Morgan's recovered memory from 12:40 AM at the party shows him sitting down across from journalist Taylor Chase with the recorder running, spilling the worst things he had on Marcus. The lobbyist who built Marcus's public narrative as the principled face of AI ethics had decided last night to feed that narrative to the press, item by item. His name never reached the suspect board this morning.

Morgan Goes on the Record

TAYLOR turns on their recorder and you begin to spill the worst things you have on MARCUS. It's a long list. Time to start with his sterling reputation…that ridiculous world leader request. MARCUS: "Merkel visited Zuckerberg. Singapore's PM flew to Cupertino for Cook. I'm building something bigger. Get me a real meeting. Someone with actual power."
Morgan Reed

"I'm worried about what Vic knows. I wish I could remember that last convo with them, but my memory is not the best."

Marcus Blackwood, recorded in Sam's diary, 2/17/27

Vic Kingsley was outside on the sidewalk by then, in her own recovered memory, pressing a designer scarf against Marcus's bleeding nose. The whiteboard preserved the line the room itself wrote down: "Alex is in, Marcus is out." The fight between Sarah and Alex this morning was a duel over a seat the investor had already begun arranging to fill.

Remi Whitman and Alex Reeves shared a moment overheard in passing. 'You found my memory,' Remi said. 'I did,' Alex replied. What Alex did with that information is the kind of thing this morning's verdict did not ask.

Flip's signed IOU to Marcus from 2020, with a loan shark texting in real time for thirty thousand dollars, never went up. Jess Kane's confirmed paternity test, 99.85%, naming Marcus as the father of her child, never went up. Jess herself, in a moment Ashe overheard between rounds, asked Blake instead: 'Should I go talk to Sarah? What do you think?' The answer stayed between them. Kai Andersen's emailed instructions from Marcus the day of the party, demanding 'environmental cues' run 'exactly as written,' never went up. And Ashe, who carried the prosecution all morning, was holding the heaviest motive of anyone present.

Editor thinks it needs "more substantiation." She'll be reassigned to lifestyle by Monday. Now about that exclusive the launch... Taylor Chase "Reality is negotiable." -Tim Ferriss

His investigation of Marcus's memory experiments was killed three weeks before the party. The journalist who arranged the burial was Taylor Chase, the same Taylor Chase whose recorder, in Morgan's recovered memory from 12:40 AM, was rolling while Morgan spilled the dirt the night Marcus died. The same name appears on both sides of the leverage. Ashe carried the prosecution. Ashe carried the heaviest motive. The room cast its vote for Quinn, and in the same hour NeurAI's offer landed. The verdict cleared the room of everyone in it.

Follow the Money

The night's burial ledger ran to $7.46 million across nine shell accounts. The verdict landed on the one suspect with no seat at the table. The burials happened in real time around twelve people who did have seats. Whatever the room concluded about Quinn's lab work, someone in the building decided several other questions were worth a lot of money to keep quiet.

The largest account, B, took in $2.73 million across seven transactions. That is by a wide margin the cleanest cover name on the ledger and the most expensive secret of the morning. Mel's recorded memory described Sarah's shared account with Marcus as 'nearly empty.' Whoever B is, they did not have that problem. Leo posted three transactions at an average of $410,000 each, the highest per-transaction value of any large account.

If chemistry was the murder weapon, the chemistry trail on the burial ledger is conspicuously cheap. The Stanford Four's lab access generated almost no observable pattern aligned to chemistry. Either the room exposed all of it, or the burials are sitting inside accounts named Squirrels, Spongebob, and President. The two cartoon-named accounts moved nearly $1.3 million in eleven transactions, a lot of small secrets dressed up to look unserious. Jamie's account is the only one that uses a roster player's actual first name. $75,009 on a single ticket. The nine-dollar tail is either bookkeeping or a tell.

Blake himself appears on his own ledger at $500,000 across a single transaction, routed at 9:21 AM into an account chosen with a label named for the Valet. Perhaps this was intended as a tip-off that Blake was there to oversee more than just a murder investigation.

What's Missing

Quinn Sterling was never in the room to defend or confess. The blackmail email is real. The PT-3A demand is real. As is his absence from the warehouse this morning. When and how he left the warehouse, and who saw him last, are open questions for the active manhunt. The next time his name appears in a document, it will be on a warrant.

Five threads got logged and dropped this morning. Who actually mixed the specific Psychotrophin-3B batch that extracted everyone's memories last night, and when. When the board authorized the recruitment Blake transacted this morning, and who drafted the post-deliberation leak. Who B is on a burial ledger that dwarfs every other account. What was in Ashe's expose before Taylor and Marcus killed it. Why Phil Kowalski's name sits on a 2020 IOU for an 'undefined favor' and appears nowhere else.

I can tell you the shape of the silence. I can't tell you what's inside it. The shape cost $7.46 million.

The accusation stands. The room concluded Quinn Sterling killed Marcus Blackwood through his control of the Psychotrophin-3A synthesis Marcus was blackmailing him to produce, and law enforcement has agreed enough to open a manhunt. The evidence supports it. So does the chemistry timeline. The verdict is not wrong on its face.

What the verdict does not account for is everyone else in that room. The succession was being settled in the same room, at the same hour. Morgan, who built Marcus's narrative as the face of principled AI, was already dismantling it on a journalist's recorder. The chemist with access to the formulation tree convicted the chemist who didn't ship the final batch. The journalist whose career Marcus destroyed led the prosecution. Flip's loan shark is still waiting. Jess's paternity test is in evidence. Kai's instructions from Marcus are still in an inbox nobody asked about. None of those people made it onto the suspect board, except in passing, and yet the motives remain real.

The system Marcus built survived him. The drug supply is still being formulated. The lobbying architecture is still being maintained. The investor who fired him before the room ever gathered still runs the board. By Monday that board will have handed Marcus's title to the partner he stole from and the wife he was divorcing. Whether they can work together remains to be seen. The technology that made it possible to extract memories from twelve people without their consent in the first place will now belong to them. The system absorbed every grievance the room declined to name.

The absent chemist absorbed the guilt that could have stuck to almost anyone in that room. Thanks to Quinn, everyone else walked out clean, and the company keeps moving.
