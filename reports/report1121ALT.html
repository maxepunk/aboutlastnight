Executive Summary

Case ID: MC-2025-568 | Status: OPEN (Homicide/Abduction) | Clearance: RESTRICTED

Marcus Blackwood is dead. That’s the headline. The visionary CEO of the hour is currently cooling in the morgue, a victim of the very underground he tried to court. The perpetrator has been identified as the Black Market representative operating under the alias "Blake Manley."

What started as a "party" at the Blackwood Warehouse was, in reality, a mass memory extraction event—a harvest. The guests were dosed, their memories stripped and locked into tokens. It appears Blackwood and Manley had a falling out. Maybe Marcus grew a conscience, or maybe he just wanted a bigger cut. Doesn't matter now. The result was a standoff that left the guests holding the keys to their own minds.

Thanks to the cooperation of the witnesses—and the relentless pressure applied by former journalist Ashe Motoko—we located the hostage site near Canyon Road. My team raided the facility. We recovered the hostages alive. But Manley? Gone. Like smoke in a wind tunnel. He left behind a corpse and a lot of questions, but at least the civilians are safe.

Detective's Note: I told these people they were missing time. They didn't listen at first. But when the adrenaline hit, they stepped up. They didn't just save their memories; they saved lives. Not bad for a group of "sleepyheads."

Evidence Locker

The scene was cluttered with the debris of high-stakes corporate warfare and domestic rot. We found thirty distinct items of interest. The following are the most critical for prosecution.

The Corporate Facade

Item: Company One-Pagers & Cease & Desist Letter (NeurAI)

Analysis: Documents recovered from the locked caches of James and Alex paint a picture of a fractured empire. The Cease & Desist letter regarding NeurAI suggests Marcus was aggressively stomping out competition—or theft—from his own former allies. The James <> Alex emails confirm a conspiracy to oust Marcus before the body was even cold.

Item: Taylor's Betrayal Printout - "Ashe Story Burial Email"

Analysis: A smoking gun for media manipulation. Taylor, an editor, buried Ashe Motoko's exposé on Blackwood’s illegal human experimentation in exchange for exclusive access. Journalism isn't dead; it was just bought and paid for by Blackwood.

Detective's Note: Everyone has a price. Taylor's was apparently an "exclusive launch." Hope it was worth the obstruction of justice charge.

Item: Silicon Valley Business Journal & Investment Summaries

Analysis: Victoria's financial trails link Blackwood to shell vehicles in the energy sector. This wasn't just about memory drugs; it was about laundering money through "innovation."

The Domestic Rot

Item: Paternity Test for Jessicah

Analysis: 99.85% probability. Marcus was the father. This adds a volatile emotional motive to the mix, though it seems unconnected to the Black Market hit.

Item: Sarah <> Rachel Texts & Diana's Divorce Emails

Analysis: Sarah Blackwood knew about the infidelity. Diana was the mistress and a divorce attorney, which is a dangerous combination of skillset and proximity. Marcus was fighting a war on the home front while inviting sharks into his living room.

Memory Analysis

The "tokens" recovered from the scene are not just souvenirs; they are witness statements frozen in silica. The tech is impressive, I'll give them that. But the content is damning.

Token ID: MAB001 - Black Market EXPIRATION lie

Content: Marcus's personal experiment notes.

Significance: This is the pivot point. Blake Manley told the hostages that unrecovered memories would be destroyed—"expired." The evidence proves this was a lie. "Expiration" means the memories restore naturally to the owner. It was a fear tactic to force compliance and sales. Manley wasn't just a killer; he was a con artist.

Token ID: DIN021 - Diana's Memory

Content: Diana overhearing a man named "Morgan" threatening Marcus regarding Senator Walsh.

Significance: Introduces a new player: Morgan. Political blackmail involving a Senator takes this out of local jurisdiction and straight into the federal swamp.

Detective's Note: Morgan. Walsh. The Black Market. We pulled on a loose thread and the whole sweater is unraveling.

Token ID: ASM042 - Ashe’s Memory 2

Content: Ashe being legally silenced by Marcus.

Significance: Establishes motive for Ashe's crusade. It wasn't just a story; it was personal retribution for a destroyed career.

Suspect Network

  • Marcus Blackwood (DECEASED): Architect of his own demise. Arrogant, brilliant, and sloppy. He thought he could control the Black Market. He was wrong.
  • "Blake Manley" (FUGITIVE): The primary antagonist. Charismatic, lethal, and currently in the wind. He executed the hit on Marcus and orchestrated the hostage situation. Caution: Armed and highly dangerous.
  • Ashe Motoko (WITNESS/ASSET): Instrumental in the resolution. Her investigative work provided the coordinates for the Canyon Road raid. She’s a pain in the ass, but she’s a useful one.
  • Victoria, James, Alex (PERSONS OF INTEREST): Corporate vultures. They didn't kill Marcus, but they were certainly sharpening their knives. We'll get them on financial crimes if the homicide charges don't stick.
  • Sarah Blackwood (WITNESS): The grieving widow, though I use "grieving" loosely. Her knowledge of Marcus's affairs makes her a key witness for the financial dissection of the Blackwood estate.

Outstanding Questions

We won the battle at the warehouse, but the war isn't over. The following leads need immediate follow-up:

  1. Where is Blake Manley? He slipped the net at Canyon Road. He has resources and likely a new face by now.
  2. Who is Morgan? The memory token referenced a "Morgan" holding a leash on Marcus. Is this the Black Market leadership? Or a third party?
  3. The Senator Walsh Connection: If a sitting Senator is involved in memory trafficking, this case just went nuclear. I need authorization to dig into Walsh's donor list.

Final Thoughts: The partygoers went home. They got their memories back—the good, the bad, and the ugly. They chose accountability over ignorance. That gives me a little hope. Not much, but enough to keep the lights on in the office for one more night.

File closed for now. But keep eyes on the shadows.
